## 1. DOCUMENT IDENTIFICATION

This is **not** a patient-initiated Release of Information (ROI) form. The document is titled "LRH PHYSICIANS GROUP RELEASE/AUTHORIZATION FORM" (document code CONS00306 (05), dated LRH 03/20) and serves as a multi-purpose intake/consent form for the physician group practice. It covers nine distinct topics across 2 pages:

1. Authorization to disclose information **to insurance carriers** for payment processing
2. Irrevocable assignment of insurance benefits and appointment as authorized representative
3. Medicaid authorization
4. Lifetime Medicare assignment authorization
5. Statement of financial responsibility
6. Authorization for medical/surgical treatment
7. Notice about physician assistants, APRNs, and CNMs
8. Release of responsibility for personal articles
9. Electronic health care records / Patient Portal acknowledgment

In context of the three forms found for Lakeland Regional Health: form-1.pdf and form-3.pdf are actual patient-facing "Authorization to Use or Disclose Protected Health Information" forms (the true ROI forms, with form-3 being the current version). Form-2 is a fundamentally different document ‚Äî it is a provider-initiated, multi-purpose intake consent bundle primarily designed to secure insurance assignment and financial responsibility, not to facilitate a patient's request for their own records.

## 4. CONTENT DESIGN

**Length**: 2 pages, approximately 11,570 characters of text. The form packs nine distinct legal sections into this space, making it extremely dense.

**Clarity**: The language is overwhelmingly legalistic. Nearly every sentence is a compound-complex construction with multiple embedded clauses. For example, Section 1 contains a single sentence of approximately 100 words: *"The undersigned hereby authorizes Lakeland Regional Health Systems, Inc. d/b/a Lakeland Regional Health Physicians Group (LRHPG) to release and obtain information from insurance carriers listed in the patient's record or any insurance carrier represented as contractually responsible for payment in whole or in part of the patient's health care bill and such diagnostic and therapeutic information and records (including any treatment or testing for mental illness, alcohol abuse, drug abuse, or HIV/AIDS) as may be necessary to determine benefits entitlement and to process payment claims for health care provided to the above-named patient."* This is well beyond a typical patient reading level.

**Organization**: Sections are numbered 1‚Äì9, which provides some structure, but there is no visual hierarchy ‚Äî each section runs directly into the next with identical formatting. The header area with patient/insurance fields is visually distinct from the legal text body, but the nine legal paragraphs are an undifferentiated wall of text.

**Signature area**: The form requires signatures from the patient, a guarantor, a co-guarantor, and two witnesses ‚Äî six signature lines total. This is an unusually heavy signature burden for an intake form.

## 5. PATIENT-CENTEREDNESS

This document is fundamentally not patient-centered for the purpose of records access:

- **Not a patient access form**: This form does not enable a patient to request their own health records. It is a provider-initiated consent form that authorizes the organization to disclose information to insurers, share records with other providers, contact the patient by phone/text, and assign insurance benefits.
- **No scope options for patient**: The patient has no ability to select what information is shared or with whom ‚Äî the authorization is blanket and covers "diagnostic and therapeutic information and records (including any treatment or testing for mental illness, alcohol abuse, drug abuse, or HIV/AIDS)."
- **No delivery format options**: No choice of paper, electronic, or portal delivery for the patient.
- **No HIPAA Right of Access reference**: The form does not reference 45 CFR ¬ß 164.524 or the patient's right to access their own records.
- **No EHI export mention**: No reference to electronic health information export or bulk data access.
- **Section 9 mentions Patient Portal**: The only patient-facing access element is Section 9, which acknowledges the existence of a "free, secure, web-based method for patients to access portions of their medical records (Patient Portal)." However, this is informational only ‚Äî it does not constitute a mechanism for requesting records.

## 6. COMPLIANCE

Several compliance-relevant observations:

- **Irrevocable assignment**: Section 2 is titled "IRREVOCABLE ASSIGNMENT FOR INSURANCE BENEFITS." The irrevocable nature of the insurance benefit assignment, combined with an instruction that "I agree to forward to LRHPG all insurance, benefits, and other third-party payments that I receive for services rendered to me immediately upon receipt," creates a strong financial obligation.
- **Broad consent bundling**: This single form bundles at least six distinct authorizations: (1) information release to insurers, (2) irrevocable insurance assignment, (3) Medicaid/Medicare authorization, (4) financial responsibility, (5) medical treatment consent, and (6) electronic communications consent. Patients sign once at the bottom to agree to all nine sections simultaneously.
- **Lifetime Medicare authorization**: Section 4 explicitly states "I understand that this is a lifetime authorization" for Medicare assignment ‚Äî an unusually long duration.
- **Collection costs and attorney's fees**: Section 5 states the patient "agrees to pay all collection expenses, including LRHPG's reasonable attorney's fees."
- **18-month insurance disclosure authorization**: Section 1's authorization for insurance information release "shall cease to be valid 18 months from this date."
- **Automated calls and texts**: Section 1 authorizes contact "by telephone (including wireless or cellular phone)... through the use of automated dialing technology and/or pre-recorded messages" and Section 1's final paragraph authorizes text messages.
- **Witness requirement**: Two witness signatures are required, which adds procedural burden.

- **Massive consent bundling**: Nine distinct legal authorizations ‚Äî ranging from treatment consent to insurance assignment to robocall consent to personal property liability waivers ‚Äî are bundled into a single form with a single signature block.
