## 1. DOCUMENT IDENTIFICATION

This is a dedicated **patient access request form** ‚Äî specifically for patients (or their legally authorized representatives) requesting access to their own protected health information under HIPAA's Right of Access. The form title is "PATIENT REQUEST FOR ACCESS TO INFORMATION," and the opening paragraph explicitly states: "A FIGHT Patient or legally authorized representative has a right to access the Patient's protected health information ('PHI'), which includes a right to inspect or copy the Patient's PHI, or both."

Philadelphia FIGHT maintains a separate companion form (form-2.pdf, "Authorization for Use and Disclosure of Information," dated 8.2024) for third-party release/disclosure. This two-form approach correctly distinguishes between a patient exercising their right of access and a patient authorizing disclosure to a third party ‚Äî a meaningful structural distinction that many organizations fail to make.

Both forms were found on the same "Forms and Consents" page (https://fight.org/about/forms-and-consents/), where form-1 was listed as "Request for Access to Information Form."

## 5. PATIENT-CENTEREDNESS

**Patient access focus:** This form is explicitly and exclusively designed for patient access requests, not third-party release. This is evident from the title, the opening rights statement, and the absence of any "release to" third-party fields. This is a best practice.

**Scope options:** The form offers three levels of granularity:
- Entire medical record (yes/no checkbox)
- Entire billing record (yes/no checkbox)
- Specific information with free-text description ("including dates, specific tests, or any other indications")

This gives patients flexibility to request exactly what they need.

**Format options:** The form explicitly offers both "Paper Copies" and "Electronic Format" with a "Specify: ___" field for the electronic format. This is good ‚Äî it acknowledges the patient's right to receive records in electronic form and allows them to name a preferred format.

**Delivery options:** Pick up or mail. No mention of email, portal, or other digital delivery mechanisms. The mail option includes fields for recipient name and address.

**Right of Access language:** The opening paragraph explicitly references the right to access PHI and the right to inspect or copy. It does not cite a specific HIPAA section number (e.g., ¬ß 164.524), but the substance is correct.

**EHI/bulk data:** No mention of electronic health information export, patient portal access, or bulk data download options.

**Representative access:** The form handles multiple requester categories well: self (adult or adolescent 13+), legal guardian/parent (with sub-options for pediatric 0-12 and adolescent 13-17), and legally authorized representative (with fields for name, DOB, and description of legal authority). The adolescent age differentiation (13+ can self-request) reflects Pennsylvania-specific consent laws.

**Submission methods:** Listed at the bottom: "in person at any Philadelphia FIGHT Health Center, via email to privacy@fight.org, via fax 215-732-1145 Attention: Privacy Officer, or via mail." This provides four submission channels including email and fax, which is good.

## 6. COMPLIANCE

**Fees/costs:** The introductory paragraph states FIGHT will charge for: "(1) labor for copying the requested PHI, whether on paper or electronic form, consistent with then current Pennsylvania law; (2) supplies for creating the paper copy or electronic media; (3) postage, when the Patient requests that FIGHT mail the information; and (4) preparation of an explanation or summary of the PHI, if agreed to by the Individual." The phrase "consistent with then current Pennsylvania law" is a reasonable framework, though no specific dollar amounts are provided. Notably, under HIPAA's Right of Access rule, fees for patient access requests are limited to reasonable cost-based fees, and the form's fee language could be read as going beyond what HIPAA permits for patient access (e.g., "labor for copying" is permissible under state law frameworks but must be constrained to "reasonable" under federal rules).

**Denial rights:** The form states: "Under some circumstances, such as increased risk of harm or injury, FIGHT may deny the Patient's request for access to PHI." This is a legitimate carve-out under HIPAA (¬ß 164.524(a)(2)-(3)), though the form does not mention the patient's right to request a review of the denial, which HIPAA requires for certain types of denials.

**Timelines:** The form correctly states 30 days to respond, with possible 60-day extension.

**No notarization requirement.** No ID verification requirements mentioned beyond the signature. No unreasonable barriers to submission.

## 7. NOTABLE OBSERVATIONS

### ‚ú® BRIGHT SPOTS

- **Separate patient access form:** Philadelphia FIGHT correctly maintains distinct forms for patient access (form-1) vs. third-party release (form-2). Many organizations force patients through a generic authorization form even for self-access requests. This is a best practice worth emulating.

- **Electronic format option:** The form explicitly offers electronic format with an open "Specify" field, letting patients name their preferred electronic format.
- **Adolescent self-access at 13+:** The form respects Pennsylvania minor consent law by allowing adolescents 13 and older to make their own access requests, with appropriate guardian/parent pathways for younger patients.
- **Bilingual availability:** Per the retrieval notes, the Forms and Consents page offers forms in both English and Spanish.
