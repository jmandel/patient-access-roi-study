## 4. CONTENT DESIGN

**Length**: One page ‚Äî appropriately concise for the information collected.

**Organization**: The form follows a logical top-to-bottom structure:
1. Provider identification
2. Patient identification (name, SSN, DOB)
3. Release recipient
4. General authorization with record type checkboxes
5. Dates of care / scope options
6. Expiration options
7. Specific authorization for sensitive conditions
8. Copy usage clause
9. Signature block
10. Facility addresses and contact info

**Clarity issues**:
- The form contains a **spelling error**: `"INFORMATION REQUIRIED"` ‚Äî should be "REQUIRED."
- The `"PROVIDER"` field label says `"Name & Address of Health Care Facility"` but doesn't clarify whether the patient should fill this in or if it's pre-populated by the facility. The newer form-1 uses the clearer label `"RELEASE FROM"`.
- The expiration section offers three options but has no default ‚Äî unlike form-1, which defaults to "1 year unless noted otherwise." A patient who fails to initial any expiration option creates an ambiguous authorization.
- The `"USE OF COPIES"` clause (`"A copy of this authorization with my signature thereon (initial one) ( ) MAY; ( ); MAY NOT be used with the same effectiveness as an original"`) places a decision on the patient that most would not understand the implications of.

**Layout**: Text is dense but readable on a single page. The parenthetical initial markers `( )` are functional but visually cluttered. Section headers like `"GENERAL AUTHORIZATION"` and `"SPECIFIC AUTHORIZATION"` provide some hierarchy but are not strongly differentiated visually.

## 5. PATIENT-CENTEREDNESS

**Patient access vs. third-party release**: This is a **general release form** designed for releasing records to a third party (`"Release TO: Name & Address of Recipient Agency, Organization or Individual to whom information is to be released"`). There is no version or pathway specifically for a patient requesting their own records. A patient would need to list themselves in the "Release To" field.

**Scope options**: The form offers several record-type options:
- Copy of H&P, Discharge Summary & Operative Reports
- Copy of Outpatient/ER records
- Copy of complete medical record
- Copy of Electronic Discharge Instructions
- "All past admissions or care at this facility" or limited to specific dates/conditions
- An "Other" write-in field

These are reasonable but clinician-oriented (patients may not know what "H&P" means).

**Format/delivery options**: None specified. No mention of electronic delivery, email, fax, or mail preferences. The newer form-1 added `"PLEASE EMAIL RECORDS IF POSSIBLE TO: MRChartPullRequest@slvrmc.org"` ‚Äî this older version lacks that entirely.

**Rights language**: No reference to HIPAA ¬ß 164.524, patient right of access, or any patient rights explanation. No mention of EHI export or electronic health information.

**SSN collection**: The form requests the patient's Social Security Number. While historically common on medical forms, this creates an unnecessary data exposure risk and may deter patients concerned about identity theft.

## 6. COMPLIANCE

**Expiration**: Three options are offered (specific date, 180 days, or purpose-based with patient-specified number of days). No default expiration is stated if the patient fails to initial any option, which could be interpreted as an indefinite authorization ‚Äî or as an invalid one. The newer form-1 solved this by defaulting to 1 year.

**Sensitive information protections**: The form appropriately requires specific authorization for drug abuse, alcohol abuse, and psychological/psychiatric conditions, with reference to federal regulations on substance abuse records (42 CFR Part 2). The note about the alcohol/drug abuse statement being attached to disclosures is a proper compliance element.

**Barriers**: No notarization requirement. No in-person submission requirement. No fees disclosed or demanded. The form is straightforward in what it requires.

**Disclaimer**: The form states `"San Luis Valley Health is not responsible if lost"` ‚Äî this vague disclaimer could be read as disclaiming responsibility for records lost in transit, which is an unusual and potentially concerning statement for a patient trusting the organization to handle their PHI.

**Contact**: Lists phone number 719-587-1392 (differs from 719-587-1391 on form-1, possibly reflecting a departmental change or error).
