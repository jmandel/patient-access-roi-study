## 6. COMPLIANCE

**Fees**: The form states: "Valleywise Health may charge a fee for the copying of requested health information plus postage for mailing the copies to you. If you would like a copy of your record to be provided on portable media such as a CD or USB drive, we may charge you the actual cost of the portable media." The use of "may charge" is hedging language; no specific fee schedule is provided. The language about "actual cost of the portable media" aligns with HIPAA's cost-based fee limit for patient access requests.

**Timeline**: "Valleywise Health will respond to your request for health information within 30 days of our receipt of your request" with a possible 30-day extension if records are not readily accessible or are off-site. This aligns with HIPAA's 30-day response requirement with one 30-day extension (¬ß 164.524(b)(2)).

**No notarization or in-person requirement**: The form does not require notarization, witnesses, or in-person submission ‚Äî a positive compliance signal.

**No expiration clause**: Unlike Form 43439 (which expires 1 year after signature if no date is specified), this patient access request form has no expiration provision. This is appropriate since it is a one-time access request, not an ongoing authorization.

**SSN collection**: The form includes a field for Social Security Number. While this may be used for patient identification, collecting SSN on a form that could be faxed or emailed raises security concerns and is not a HIPAA requirement for patient access requests.

## 7. NOTABLE OBSERVATIONS

### ‚ú® BRIGHT SPOTS

- **Dedicated patient access form**: Valleywise Health maintains a separate form specifically for patient access requests, distinct from the third-party release authorization. This is a best practice that many organizations fail to implement.
- **Full bilingual (English/Spanish) design**: The entire form is duplicated in Spanish on page 2 ‚Äî not just a translation notice, but a complete parallel form. Given the Phoenix, AZ demographics, this is highly appropriate and well-executed.
- **Granular record type selection**: 16+ specific record types allow patients to request exactly what they need rather than forcing an all-or-nothing choice.
- **Email encryption transparency**: The form proactively addresses email security, defaults to encryption, and gives patients an informed choice to opt out ‚Äî a thoughtful approach to balancing security and convenience.
- **MyChart portal option**: Including the patient portal as a delivery method offers a modern, secure digital pathway.
