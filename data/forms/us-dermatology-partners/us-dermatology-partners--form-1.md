**Patient access vs. third-party release:** The form bundles both purposes into a single document. "Patient Access" is one checkbox option among several under "Purpose of Use/Disclosure," but the overall form framing ("I hereby authorize use or disclosure") and structure is oriented toward third-party release. A patient requesting their own records under HIPAA's Right of Access is using the same form as an attorney requesting records for litigation ‚Äî an authorization is not required for patient access under HIPAA, though organizations commonly use them.

**Delivery options:** Three options are offered:

**Rights disclosures:** The form includes five bullet points explaining patient rights:

- Release of liability for U.S. Dermatology Partners
- Notice that records may include sensitive information (STDs, AIDS/HIV, behavioral/mental health, alcohol/drug abuse)

## 6. COMPLIANCE

**Fee language:** The form states patients are "entitled to inspect my records and that a reasonable fee may be charged for the records." This is vague ‚Äî it does not specify what the fee covers, what amount is considered "reasonable," or that HIPAA limits fees for patient-access requests to reasonable cost-based fees. The phrase "a reasonable fee may be charged" could lead patients to believe fees are always applicable, even for patient-access requests where HIPAA limits what can be charged.

**Liability waiver:** The form includes: "I release U.S. Dermatology Partners from any legal responsibility or liability for disclosure that may arise as a result of the use of the information contained in the Protected Health Information." This is a broad liability release that patients must agree to as part of the authorization ‚Äî there is no opt-out.

**Continuing disclosure option:** The form offers a "Continuing Disclosure for 12 months" option, which is reasonable and within typical timeframes.

**Submission method:** The form does not specify how it should be submitted (mail, fax, in-person, email). This is an ambiguity ‚Äî patients have the form but may not know where to send it.

**Revocation:** The revocation language is appropriate: "I may revoke this authorization in writing at any time, except to the extent that release has been made prior to my revocation."
