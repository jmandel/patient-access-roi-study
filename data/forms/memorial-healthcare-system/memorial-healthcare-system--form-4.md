## 5. PATIENT-CENTEREDNESS

**Strengths:**
- The form clearly states the patient's right to request amendments, which is itself an exercise in patient education.
- It explains what will and won't happen: "Medical records will be amended only through the addition of information... Existing records will not be removed, destroyed, or altered."
- It provides specific guidance on how to request amendments during treatment ("the request may be made to the provider who created the record").
- A privacy office email (MHSPRIVACY@mhs.net) is provided as a contact point.

**Weaknesses:**
- This form is only available in English. Unlike the ROI form (available in English, Spanish, and Haitian Creole), the amendment form has no translated versions, creating an access barrier for the same multilingual South Florida population that the ROI forms serve.
- No electronic submission option is mentioned. The form says to "complete the top section of this form and send it to" the legal/privacy office, but doesn't specify whether email submission of the completed form is acceptable or if it must be physically mailed.
- There is no mention of timelines for the organization's response, even though HIPAA requires action within 60 days.
- The form lacks any reference to the patient's right to submit a statement of disagreement if the amendment is denied.

**Scope**: This form is specifically for amendment requests, not record access. It does not overlap with or substitute for the ROI authorization form (form-1). It does not reference HIPAA's Right of Access or any connection to requesting copies of records.

## 6. COMPLIANCE

- **Amendment denial grounds**: The listed denial reasons align with HIPAA ¬ß 164.526(a)(2), which permits denial when information wasn't created by the entity, isn't part of the designated record set, isn't available for inspection, or is accurate and complete. The form also correctly notes denial is permitted if "not in writing or does not include a reason to support the request."
- **No mention of patient recourse**: HIPAA ¬ß 164.526(d) requires that if an amendment is denied, the covered entity must inform the patient of their right to submit a statement of disagreement and to have the request and denial appended to future disclosures. This form omits any such information.
- **No processing timeline**: HIPAA requires the entity to act on amendment requests within 60 days (with a possible 30-day extension). The form does not mention any timeline.
- **Submission channel ambiguity**: The form provides a mailing address and an email address but does not clearly state whether the form can be submitted via email, potentially creating confusion about acceptable submission methods.
