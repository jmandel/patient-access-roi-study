## 4. CONTENT DESIGN

**Length:** One page ‚Äî appropriately concise for an authorization form.

**Organization:** The form uses a clear numbered structure with six sections:
1. Patient demographics
2. Delivery destination and method
3. Record type selection
4. Purpose of disclosure
5. Fee schedule
6. Patient signature and legal disclosures

This logical flow is well-designed ‚Äî it moves from "who" to "where" to "what" to "why" to "cost" to "sign."

**Clarity:** The form uses relatively plain language. Section headers like "TELL US ABOUT THE PATIENT" and "WHERE AND HOW ARE WE SENDING THE RECORDS?" are conversational and patient-friendly. However, the legal disclosures in Section 6 shift to dense HIPAA boilerplate ("I understand information used/disclosed pursuant to this authorization may be subject to re-disclosure by the recipient..."), which is standard but less accessible.

**Layout:** Fitting six sections plus legal text onto a single page makes it somewhat dense. The delivery options (a. mail, b. email, c. fax) are organized clearly with an instruction to "PLEASE CHOOSE ONLY ONE OPTION (A, B OR C)." The record type section offers checkboxes for specific provider selection, date ranges, specific conditions, and record subtypes (labs, radiology, office notes, immunizations, operative reports).

**Readability:** Three fonts are used, and based on the text density, font size is likely adequate but tight given the single-page constraint.

## 5. PATIENT-CENTEREDNESS

**Patient access vs. third-party release:** The form does not distinguish between these two use cases. The "Purpose of Disclosure" section offers "Personal Use," "Transfer/Continuity of Care," "Litigation/Legal," and "Other." Selecting "Personal Use" effectively signals a Right of Access request, though the form never explicitly references HIPAA ¬ß 164.524 or a patient's right of access.

**Scope options:** Good granularity. Patients can select:
- Specific providers or "All Clinic Providers"
- Associate practices (with a fill-in field)
- Date ranges or "All dates of treatment"
- Specific conditions/injuries
- Record subtypes: Ambulatory Surgery Center Records, Labs/Path Only, Radiology Reports Only, Office Notes Only, Immunization Records, Other
- Sensitive records: "Records including mental health, HIV, and/or substance abuse records (cross out any item you do not authorize disclosure.)"

**Delivery options:** Three methods are offered:
- a. Mail to an address
- b. Email
- c. Fax (marked "Healthcare Providers Only")

Email availability for patients is a positive. However, fax is restricted to healthcare providers, and there is no mention of electronic portal access, patient portal download, or EHI export.

**Rights language:** The signature section includes standard HIPAA language about the right to revoke, non-conditioning of treatment on signing, and re-disclosure risk. It does not explicitly inform patients of their right to access their own records under HIPAA.

**EHI/bulk data:** No mention of electronic health information export, USCDI, or bulk data access.

## 6. COMPLIANCE

**Fee schedule:** Section 5 states: "Per KRS 422.317, patients are entitled to the first copy of their medical record free of charge. Each additional copy shall be $1.00 per page." It also notes "Records transferred directly to another healthcare entity are free of charge" and "There is no additional charge for records emailed, faxed or picked up at facility." Fees are processed by VitalChart, not Lexington Clinic directly. The Kentucky statute reference is appropriate, and the first-copy-free provision is patient-friendly.

**Late fees:** The form states "payments received after 30 days are subject to $5.00 late fee" and "fees are non-refundable once services are rendered." This applies to the VitalChart invoice.

**Expiration:** "This authorization expires in 1 year from date of signature unless a specific date/event is listed." One year is standard and reasonable.

**Submission:** The form lists submission via mail to "Lexington Clinic Release of Information c/o VitalChart ¬∑ 791 Freight Blvd., Suite 150 ¬∑ Lexington, KY 40511" with phone, fax, and email contact info for VitalChart. The form itself does not explicitly state *how* to submit it (mail, fax, email, or in-person), but the VitalChart contact info implies these channels are available.

**Employee restriction:** "LC Employees: This authorization does not permit usage of our computer systems to access your or a family member's PHI." This is an internal compliance safeguard.

**SSN collection:** The form asks for SSN (formatted as "XXX-XX-____" suggesting last 4 digits) and MRN. Collecting even partial SSN for a records release is worth noting.
