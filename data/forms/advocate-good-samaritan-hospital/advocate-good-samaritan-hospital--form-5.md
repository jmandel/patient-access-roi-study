## 1. DOCUMENT IDENTIFICATION

This is the **general third-party authorization form** for releasing protected health information from Advocate Health facilities, including Advocate Good Samaritan Hospital. It is a system-wide form used across both Illinois and Wisconsin (as indicated by the filename suffix "wi-il"). The form's title ‚Äî "Authorization to Release Protected Health Information" ‚Äî and its structure (requiring a named recipient under "Release the Requested Information to") make clear it is designed for disclosing PHI to third parties, not specifically for a patient requesting their own records.

Advocate Health provides a separate companion form for patient self-requests: **form-4.pdf** ("Patient Request for Health Information," form code S88411), which includes a "Myself" checkbox option, a callout to the LiveWell patient portal, and omits the third-party authorization language. The two forms share a nearly identical structure for selecting record types and delivery methods but serve different purposes: form-4 is patient-access-specific, while form-5 is the standard ROI authorization for disclosures to others.

Other forms found for this organization include: an older Advocate Medical Group (AMG) version of a release form (form-2), two Employee Assistance Program forms (form-1, form-3), a Spanish-language version of a related authorization (form-6, form code S23623-SP, 3 pages), and an instructions document (form-7). The Spanish version (form-6) appears to correspond to an older version of the authorization form rather than being a direct translation of form-5, since its form code (S23623) differs from form-5's (S300503) and it uses a different layout with numbered sections.

## 4. CONTENT DESIGN

**Length and density:** The entire form fits on a single page, which is commendable for a form covering medical records, imaging, billing, delivery method, purpose, rights language, and signature blocks. However, this compression comes at a cost ‚Äî the form is quite dense. The text extracted runs to 3,423 characters with minimal white space implied by the layout.

**Organization:** The form follows a logical flow: patient identification ‚Üí treatment facility/dates ‚Üí behavioral health mutual exchange checkbox ‚Üí record type selection (Medical Records / Imaging / Billing) ‚Üí recipient information ‚Üí purpose of release ‚Üí delivery method ‚Üí rights statement ‚Üí expiration ‚Üí signature. **Record type granularity:** The form offers detailed checkboxes under three categories:

**Delivery method options:** The form lists multiple options:

The inclusion of encrypted email and patient portal as delivery options is notable and modern.

**Readability concern:** The single-page compression means font sizes are likely small. With 3,423 characters of text, detailed checkbox groups, multiple signature blocks, and rights language all on one page, readability may be challenging for some patients.

## 5. PATIENT-CENTEREDNESS

**Patient access vs. third-party release:** This form is designed for **third-party disclosures**, not patient self-access. The "Release the Requested Information to" section requires a named recipient with address, phone, and fax ‚Äî there is no "Myself" checkbox as found on the companion form-4. A patient wanting their own records would need to use form-4 instead. The medical records webpage does differentiate the two forms, but the form itself does not direct patients to the correct form if they are requesting their own records.

**Purpose options:** The form offers four purpose checkboxes: "Continuation of Care," "Personal (at my request)," "Legal/Investigation," and "Insurance," plus an "Other" field. The "Personal (at my request)" option is available but this form's structure still routes records to a named third-party recipient.

**Format/delivery options:** Strong. The form offers paper mail, CD, encrypted email, patient portal, and in-person pick-up. The "(Fees may apply)" note is honest but vague ‚Äî it does not specify fee amounts.

**Right of Access language:** The form does not explicitly reference HIPAA ¬ß 164.524 or the patient's Right of Access. The rights section focuses on revocation rights, non-conditioning of treatment on signing, and re-disclosure risks ‚Äî all standard authorization elements. There is no mention of EHI (Electronic Health Information) export, bulk data, or the 21st Century Cures Act.

**Sensitive information handling:** The form states: "This is a full release, including information related to behavioral/mental health, drug and alcohol abuse treatment (in compliance with 42 CFR Part 2), genetic information, HIV/AIDS, and other sexually transmitted diseases." This is an opt-in-everything approach ‚Äî there are no checkboxes to exclude sensitive categories (unlike the Spanish-language form-6/S23623, which allows patients to check categories to exclude). This is a significant difference from the companion forms.

**Minor authorization:** Includes a thoughtful section on minors' right to sign for certain treatment types (pregnancy, STDs, outpatient behavioral health, substance use), which reflects Illinois law requirements.

## 6. COMPLIANCE

**Expiration:** "This authorization expires one year from the date of my signature unless otherwise written here." One year is a standard and reasonable expiration period.

**Fees:** The form notes "(Fees may apply)" next to the delivery method section but does not disclose specific amounts. This is somewhat concerning ‚Äî HIPAA limits fees for patient access to reasonable cost-based fees, and patients should ideally know costs upfront.

**No notarization requirement:** The form does not require notarization.

**No in-person-only restriction:** The form offers mail, fax, and electronic submission pathways (implicitly, as it lists a mailing destination and fax in the instructions form-7).

**Witness requirement:** Not required on this form (unlike some Illinois mental health-specific requirements addressed in the Spanish form-6).

**42 CFR Part 2 compliance:** The form explicitly references 42 CFR Part 2 for substance abuse treatment records, showing awareness of federal substance use privacy protections.

**Full release default:** The statement "This is a full release, including information related to behavioral/mental health, drug and alcohol abuse treatment... genetic information, HIV/AIDS" means that signing this form releases all sensitive categories by default. Unlike the older AMG form (form-2), which lets patients opt out of specific sensitive categories, and unlike the Spanish version (form-6/S23623), which lets patients exclude sensitive categories, form-5 does not offer granular control over sensitive information types.

- **Full release of sensitive information with no opt-out mechanism.** The form states: "This is a full release, including information related to behavioral/mental health, drug and alcohol abuse treatment (in compliance with 42 CFR Part 2), genetic information, HIV/AIDS, and other sexually transmitted diseases."
