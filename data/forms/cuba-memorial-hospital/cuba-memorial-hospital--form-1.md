**Dual-purpose design**: The form title ‚Äî "AUTHORIZATION FOR PATIENT ACCESS AND/OR USE AND DISCLOSURE" ‚Äî signals it covers both patient access and third-party release. Section 4 includes explicit patient access options: "I am interested in ‚òê inspection or ‚òê copies of my medical record" and a separate authorization line: "I authorize [name] to access my medical/hospital records for inspection and/or copies." **Delivery format**: No mention of electronic delivery options. The form only offers "inspection" or "copies" (implying paper). The statement "Photo ID is required for records to be picked up" implies in-person pickup is the expected (and possibly only) delivery method.

**No fee disclosure**: Section 5(e) mentions "a reasonable copy fee" but provides no specifics about cost.

**No mention of patient rights under HIPAA ¬ß 164.524**, timelines for response, or electronic health information export.

**Expiration**: Section 6 states the authorization "will expire 90 days from the date of signature unless you request an earlier date or event." A 90-day expiration is short but not unreasonable for an authorization form; however, for a patient access request, an expiration is arguably unnecessary since patients have an ongoing right to their records.

## 6. COMPLIANCE

- **Witness requirement**: The form includes lines for "Witness" and "Witness/Interpreter (if needed)" with date and time fields. While not labeled as mandatory, the presence of a dedicated witness line with date/time could deter patients from completing the form without a witness present, which HIPAA does not require.
- **Photo ID for pickup**: "**Photo ID is required for records to be picked up.**" ‚Äî bolded and emphatic. While identity verification is reasonable, this implies in-person pickup is the standard or only method.
- **90-day expiration**: A built-in 90-day expiration may be unnecessarily restrictive for patient access requests, where HIPAA grants an ongoing right.
- **Specially protected information**: Section 7 requires separate consent checkboxes for HIV/AIDS, psychiatric/mental health, and substance abuse information ‚Äî consistent with New York State law and 42 CFR Part 2.
- **"Staff Use Only" section**: Includes "Unable to release ____ due to ____" ‚Äî suggesting denials are documented, though no appeal process is mentioned.
