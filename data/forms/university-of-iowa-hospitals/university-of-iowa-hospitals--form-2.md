## 4. CONTENT DESIGN

**Length**: 1 page ‚Äî appropriate for its purpose. The form is compact and reasonably organized.

**Structure**: The form follows a logical top-to-bottom flow: patient identification ‚Üí recipient ‚Üí format ‚Üí record types ‚Üí date range ‚Üí reason ‚Üí consent language ‚Üí sensitive categories ‚Üí expiration ‚Üí signature. This is a sensible ordering.

**Clarity**: The consent language is moderately dense but covers necessary ground. The sentence "If this consent is cancelled, I understand that information may have been released prior to the cancellation, and that action would not be considered a breach of confidentiality" is clear and appropriately worded. The form does not use plain-language summaries or explanatory headers for each section.

**Record types**: Offers 15 record type checkboxes (summary of record, immunization record, pathology slides, billing information, laboratory results, psychotherapy notes, discharge notes, office visit notes, radiology images, emergency notes, operative/procedure reports, radiology reports, history and physical, pathology reports, test results) plus an "Other" free-text field. This is a comprehensive selection.

**Date range**: Defaults to "previous two years unless specified below" with a date range field. This is a reasonable default.

**Sensitive categories**: Includes opt-out checkboxes for substance abuse, mental health, HIV-related information, and genetic tests/info, with appropriate footnotes explaining 42 CFR Part 2 protections and the scope of genetic testing.

## 5. PATIENT-CENTEREDNESS

**Patient access vs. third-party release**: The form title includes "Right of Access Request," and the recipient field includes a "Myself at the address above unless noted below" option. However, this version has been pre-populated to send records to Safer Driver Solutions, which undermines the patient-access pathway. A patient using this form for their own access would need to override the pre-filled recipient, which may not be obvious.

**Format options**: Lists electronic (CD/USB drive/MyChart), fax, verbal, email, paper, and "to file only." **Response time commitment**: "UIHC will respond to this request within 30 days of receipt. If additional time is required, you will be notified of the extension." This matches HIPAA's 30-day response requirement.

**Fee disclosure**: "I understand there may be a charge for this information." This is vague ‚Äî no fee schedule or cost estimate is provided.

**No mention of HIPAA ¬ß 164.524**, patient rights to access, or EHI export/bulk data options.

## 6. COMPLIANCE

**Conditioning treatment on authorization**: The form states "UIHC does not require completion of this form as a condition of evaluation or treatment" ‚Äî an important and compliant disclosure. However, it adds an exception: "when the requested evaluation or treatment is solely for the purpose of creating a medical report for a third party, if authorization to release the information to that third party is not provided, it may result in the cancellation of those services." This exception is legally appropriate.

**Cancellation process**: Requires "written notification to the Director of Health Information Management" ‚Äî this is a standard process, though it could be simpler (e.g., email or phone cancellation).

**Future information release**: "This agreement allows release of past and future UIHC information" ‚Äî this means signing once authorizes ongoing disclosure for the duration of the authorization period (2 years). This is a broad scope that patients may not fully appreciate.

**Witness requirement**: "Witness signature, only required when patient or person legally authorized is physically unable to sign" ‚Äî this is reasonable and appropriately limited.

**No notarization required. No in-person-only submission requirement.** Submission channels include email, fax, and mail.
