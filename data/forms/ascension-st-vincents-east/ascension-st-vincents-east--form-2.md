## 4. CONTENT DESIGN

**Length**: 2 pages ‚Äî proportionate for the content covered.

**Organization**: The form follows a logical flow:
1. Opening authorization statement and sensitive-information disclosure notice
2. Patient demographics
3. Recipient designation (self or third party)
4. Location selection
5. Psychiatric/substance use records opt-in
6. Date range
7. Record type selection
8. Delivery method selection
9. Acknowledgment initials (revocation rights, non-conditioning)
10. Expiration date and signature

**Clarity**: The opening paragraph is dense: "I hereby authorize the use or disclosure of my protected health information ("PHI") as described below. This request includes any information relating to drug, alcohol use/treatment, communications with psychiatrists or psychologists, and records pertaining to sexually transmitted diseases, if they are a part of my medical record." This front-loads complex legal concepts before the patient even fills in their name. The sentence about re-disclosure ("Once this information has been disclosed, it may be subject to re-disclosure and no longer be protected by federal regulations") is standard but adds to the legal density.

The record type section is well-structured, offering a sensible hierarchy: "Key Clinical Notes" (a pre-defined bundle), "Entire Medical Record," or specific individual report types. The parenthetical "(excluding billing records, Fetal Monitoring, and Radiology images)" under "Entire Medical Record" is a helpful clarification.

**Layout**: The form uses horizontal rules to separate major sections. Checkbox-style selectors are used throughout. The delivery section on page 2 is laid out in a two-column format (options on the left, electronic records notice on the right), which is a reasonable use of space.

**Font size**: Based on the clean text extraction and the amount of content fitting on 2 pages, text appears to be at a readable size (likely 10pt or above).

**Initialing requirements**: The form requires patients to initial two separate statements about revocation rights and non-conditioning of treatment. These statements are lengthy ‚Äî the non-conditioning statement includes three bullet-pointed exceptions. While legally thorough, requiring a patient to initial these dense paragraphs creates friction and may not improve comprehension.

## 5. PATIENT-CENTEREDNESS

**Patient access vs. third-party release**: The form explicitly includes a checkbox for "me for my personal use" alongside "the party indicated below." This is a meaningful improvement over many ROI forms that are exclusively third-party-oriented. The legacy form-1 lists "Patient / Personal Representative" as one of several purpose checkboxes but frames the entire form as a disclosure authorization *to* a receiving facility.

**Scope options**: Patients can specify:
- Specific UAB St. Vincent's locations (6 options including East)
- Date range (from/to or specific date), with a default of "past 12 months" if unspecified
- Whether to include psychiatric/substance use records (Yes/No checkbox)
- Record type with three tiers: curated key clinical notes, entire medical record, or specific report types (10 options)

**Format/delivery options**: The form offers five delivery methods:
- Paper mailed to address on form
- Paper pickup by a named person
- Fax
- CD (mailed only)
- Email

The email/CD option includes a clear notice about encryption: "I understand that the records will be encrypted to help protect my privacy and the security of my health records and that I will be furnished with the information on how to access those encrypted records." This is both transparent and patient-friendly.

**Right of Access language**: The form does **not** reference HIPAA ¬ß 164.524 or the patient's Right of Access. This is a notable omission ‚Äî the older form-1 explicitly states "I may inspect or obtain a copy of the information to be used or disclosed, as provided in CFR 164.524." The newer form drops this reference.

**EHI Export**: No mention of electronic health information export, bulk data, or TEFCA.

**Fees**: No fee information is disclosed on the form. Patients are not told what, if anything, records will cost.

## 6. COMPLIANCE

**Expiration**: "This Authorization will expire on: _____. If I fail to specify an expiration date or event, this Authorization will expire six months from the date on which it was signed." A 6-month default expiration is standard and not unreasonably short for a single-use records request.

**Revocation**: The form addresses revocation rights clearly, requiring the patient to initial acknowledgment. Revocation must be provided "in writing" to "the entity privacy coordinator" ‚Äî this is standard.

**Non-conditioning**: The form requires the patient to initial a statement that treatment will not be conditioned on signing, with appropriate exceptions for research, health plan enrollment, and third-party-requested treatment. This is compliant with HIPAA requirements.

**No notarization requirement**: Unlike form-1, which includes a "For External Use Only" section listing identity verification methods including "Notarized signature," form-2 has no notarization requirement or identity verification section visible on the form.

**No witness requirement**: Form-2 requires only the patient's signature. Form-1 required both patient signature and a witness signature.

**Sensitive records opt-in**: The form asks patients to affirmatively choose whether to include psychiatric/substance use records, with a Yes/No checkbox. This is compliant with 42 CFR Part 2 for substance use records and state-level psychiatric record protections.

**Broad initial authorization language**: The opening paragraph states that the request "includes any information relating to drug, alcohol use/treatment, communications with psychiatrists or psychologists, and records pertaining to sexually transmitted diseases, if they are a part of my medical record." This broadly sweeping statement at the top of the form appears to conflict with the later Yes/No checkbox for psychiatric/substance use records ‚Äî the opening seems to include them by default while the checkbox offers a choice.
