It uses the structure "I AUTHORIZE [facility] TO DISCLOSE TO [recipient]" ‚Äî designed for sending records to another party, not for the patient requesting their own records. St. Joseph's has a separate dedicated patient access form (form-2.pdf) that is specifically titled "Patient's Request for Access to Protected Health Information" and is linked from their medical records page.

**Scope options:** Patients can specify record types via checkboxes and provide date ranges. The "ALL RECORDS" option is available. Sensitive categories (mental health, substance abuse, HIV, genetic testing) require separate initialing, which is legally appropriate.

**Format/delivery options:** None specified on the authorization form itself. The Mercy Gilbert instruction sheet (page 3) mentions walk-in pickup, mail, or having someone else pick up with a photocopy of the patient's photo ID. No electronic delivery options are mentioned.

It references HIPAA indirectly through the re-disclosure language. However, it does not cite 45 CFR ¬ß 164.524 or frame the form in terms of the patient's right of access ‚Äî because it is not a right-of-access form.

## 6. COMPLIANCE

**Expiration:** "This authorization will automatically expire one (1) year from the date of execution unless a different event or end date is specified." One year is a standard and reasonable expiration period.

**Revocation:** The form states patients may revoke "at any time" in writing, which is appropriate. However, the revocation addresses are for Chandler and Gilbert facilities: "Medical Records 475 S. Dobson Rd., Chandler, AZ 85224 or Medical Records 3555 S. Val Vista Drive, Gilbert, AZ 85297." Neither address is St. Joseph's Hospital in Phoenix.

**Re-disclosure warning:** The form includes appropriate re-disclosure language for both general PHI and 42 C.F.R. Part 2 substance abuse records.

**"Failure to provide all information" warning:** The form opens with "Failure to provide all information requested may invalidate this authorization." While technically a reasonable statement, it could discourage patients from submitting partially completed forms, even when the missing information is nonessential.

**Fees:** No fee information is provided on the authorization form itself.

**Submission methods:** The Mercy Gilbert instruction page mentions in-person pickup, mail, and proxy pickup. No electronic submission options are described for the form itself.
