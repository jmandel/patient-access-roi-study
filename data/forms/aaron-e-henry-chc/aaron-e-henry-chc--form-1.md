This document is **not a Release of Information (ROI) form**. It is a "MISSISSIPPI CHILDREN'S HEALTH PROJECT PARENT/GUARDIAN CONSENT FORM" ‚Äî a consent-to-treat form authorizing Aaron E. Henry Community Health Services Center (AEH) to provide medical, dental, and vision services to children through a mobile medical unit visiting schools. The form was the only downloadable PDF found during an extensive search; no actual ROI or medical records authorization form was located online for this organization.

The form is a 2-page fillable PDF (67,351 bytes) hosted on a third-party site (pcmac.org, the North Panola School District website), not on AEH's own website. It collects child demographics, health history, insurance information, and parental consent for school-based healthcare services.

Notably, item 7 of the consent section includes a release-of-information clause: "Release of my child's health information from AEHCHC to your child's school district upon request." This is a one-directional, bundled release embedded within the broader consent-to-treat ‚Äî it is not a patient-initiated records access mechanism.

## 4. CONTENT DESIGN

**Length**: 2 pages ‚Äî appropriate for a consent-to-treat form that also collects health history. Page 1 covers child demographics, insurance, and provider information. Page 2 covers health history, family health history, and the consent/signature block.

**Clarity**: The opening uses accessible, encouraging language: "GREAT HEALTH MAKES GREAT MINDS!!!" and "It's tough for a child to pay attention and do well in school if he/she isn't healthy." The bullet points explaining benefits ("No long wait times," "No need to miss school or work," "Access to a healthcare provider") are clear and parent-friendly.

**Organization**: The form follows a logical flow ‚Äî introduction ‚Üí child information ‚Üí health history ‚Üí family history ‚Üí consent. Section headers like "CHILD's INFORMATION," "CHILDS' HEALTH HISTORY," and "LEGAL GUARDIAN CONSENT AND ACKNOWLEDGEMENT" provide clear structure.

**Minor issues**: There is an apostrophe inconsistency ("CHILD's INFORMATION" on page 1 vs. "CHILDS' HEALTH HISTORY" on page 2). The form collects Social Security numbers for both the child and parent/guardian, which is notable from a data minimization standpoint.

## 5. PATIENT-CENTEREDNESS

**This form does not serve a patient trying to access their own health records.** It is a consent-to-treat form for school-based pediatric services. There is no mechanism for a patient (or parent/guardian) to request copies of records, specify record types or date ranges, choose a delivery format, or direct where records should be sent.

The embedded release clause (item 7: "Release of my child's health information from AEHCHC to your child's school district upon request") is a one-way, provider-to-school release ‚Äî not a patient access pathway. It does not reference HIPAA ¬ß 164.524, patient rights, or any right-of-access framework.

The form does not mention electronic delivery options, EHI export, or any way to obtain records. The form provides phone numbers ‚Äî "(662) 645-6008 or (662) 624-4292" ‚Äî but these are for questions about the school-based health program, not for medical records requests.

## 6. COMPLIANCE

**Records release bundling**: Item 7 bundles a release of the child's health information to the school district into the overall consent-to-treat form. A parent cannot consent to treatment without also consenting to the information release. This is a form of consent bundling ‚Äî though it may be permissible under the school health program's framework, it does not give the parent/guardian a separate choice on the release component.

**SSN collection**: The form requests Social Security numbers for the child, parent/guardian, and insured person. While SSNs may be needed for insurance billing, collecting the child's SSN as a required field raises data minimization concerns.

**Signature requirement**: "Must be signed in order to be seen" ‚Äî this is appropriate for a consent-to-treat form but is stated as an absolute gate to receiving services.

**No mention of HIPAA**: The form contains no HIPAA authorization language, no privacy notice reference, and no statement of patient rights. For a consent-to-treat form, a HIPAA authorization may not be required, but the bundled information release to the school district (item 7) arguably constitutes a use/disclosure that would benefit from clearer HIPAA framing.
