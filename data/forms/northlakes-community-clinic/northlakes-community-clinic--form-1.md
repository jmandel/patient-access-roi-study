## 1. DOCUMENT IDENTIFICATION

This is a one-page "Release of Information" form, identified as REG-002 / NLCC-ExtROI0-1223 and dated December 2023. It is the newer of two ROI forms published by NorthLakes Community Clinic. The companion document (form-2.pdf, "Health Information Release," dated 4/2022) is an older fillable version covering similar ground. form-1.pdf appears to be the current primary external ROI form, superseding form-2.pdf based on its more recent date and updated filename convention.

The form is a general-purpose authorization covering both patient-directed and third-party releases. It does not differentiate between a patient requesting their own records under HIPAA's Right of Access and a patient authorizing disclosure to a third party.

## 4. CONTENT DESIGN

**Structure and Organization**: The form uses a numbered section layout (1‚Äì6) covering Personal Information, Release Purpose, Release FROM, Release TO, Release Type, and Authorization/Signature. The logical flow is reasonable ‚Äî patient identification first, then directionality, then content selection, then consent.

**Density**: All content is compressed onto a single page, which makes it compact but visually crowded. The sections for release type (6a for Health Records and 6b for Mental Health/Substance Use/Psychiatry) are particularly dense, with small fonts and tightly packed checkboxes.

**Clarity of language**: The authorization statements (the bullet-pointed consent section) are reasonably clear, using "I understand that..." constructions rather than opaque legal jargon. Key rights are articulated:
- "The disclosure of this health information is voluntary and I may decline to sign this authorization."
- "NorthLakes Community Clinic will not condition treatment on whether I sign this authorization."
- "I have a right to request a copy of the signed authorization and to inspect and receive a copy of the information disclosed."

**Record selection**: The form offers granular record-type checkboxes including Progress Notes, Lab Reports/Pathology, Medication List, Radiology Reports, Radiology Images, HIV/AIDS, STD, Entire Medical File, Entire Dental File, and OT/PT/ST Evaluation. Mental health and substance use records are separated into section 6b with their own date range and sub-categories (Progress Notes, Assessment/Evaluation, Discharge Summary, Entire File) for each of Mental Health, Substance Use Treatment, and Psychiatry.

**Date ranges**: Section 6a includes a start/end date range and notes "For continued care the last 2 years of records will be sent unless a different time frame is specified." Section 6b marks its date range as "REQUIRED."

## 5. PATIENT-CENTEREDNESS

**Patient access vs. third-party release**: The form does not distinguish between these use cases. The "Release FROM" and "Release TO" structure frames the interaction as moving records between organizations, not as a patient exercising their Right of Access. A patient requesting their own records would need to put NorthLakes in the "FROM" field and themselves in the "TO" field ‚Äî workable but not intuitive for self-directed requests.

**Delivery options**: The "Release TO" section includes an email address field with the note "(NorthLakes will use encrypted email for security purposes)," which is a positive digital delivery option. The primary submission channel is fax, with an urgent fax number also provided ("For urgent requests requiring a response within a week, please fax to 715-318-5635").

**Scope options**: Good. Patients can select specific record types, specify date ranges, and choose between health records and behavioral health/substance use records separately. The "Verbal Communication" checkbox offers an alternative to written record transfer.

**Right of Access language**: The form does not explicitly reference HIPAA ¬ß 164.524 or the patient's Right of Access by name. It references "Federal Privacy Law (42 CFR Part 2) (HIPAA)" and "HIPAA 45 CFR" in the context of re-disclosure warnings and substance use record protections, but these citations are about confidentiality protections rather than patient access rights.

**EHI Export / bulk data**: No mention of electronic health information export, USCDI, or bulk data access.

**Purpose options**: The form offers purpose checkboxes including "Continued Care," "Disability," "Legal," "School," "Transfer Care," "Work Comp," and "Self/Personal." The inclusion of "Self/Personal" is positive for patient-directed requests.

## 6. COMPLIANCE

**Expiration**: "This authorization will expire one year from my signature date unless I revoke it." One year is a reasonable and standard expiration period.

**Revocation**: The form states the authorization "may be revoked at any time by providing written notice of revocation to NorthLakes Community Clinic, except when information has already been released." This is standard.

**Treatment conditioning**: The form explicitly states "NorthLakes Community Clinic will not condition treatment on whether I sign this authorization," which is correct and required.

**Minor consent requirements**: The form specifies dual-signature requirements for minors: "For Substance Use Treatment patients 12 and older, both patient and parent/legal guardian must sign" and "For Mental Health and Psychiatry patients 14 and older, both patient and parent/legal guardian must sign." These align with Wisconsin state law.

**Re-disclosure warning**: The form warns that "Information used or disclosed pursuant to this authorization may be subject to re-disclosure by the recipient and may no longer be protected by the Federal Privacy Law (42 CFR Part 2) (HIPAA)." This is standard.

**No notarization, no fee disclosure, no in-person requirement**: The form does not impose notarization, does not mention fees, and does not require in-person submission. The fax-based workflow is the default but not framed as the only option.
