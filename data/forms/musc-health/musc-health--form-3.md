## 4. CONTENT DESIGN

### Header and Routing
The form opens with clear submission instructions at the top:
- "Fax completed form to: 843-792-7009"
- "Email completed form to: cdmimages@musc.edu"

This is helpful ‚Äî the patient immediately knows where to send the form. However, these dental-specific contact details differ from both the current dental contacts listed on form-1 (cdm-roi-auth@musc.edu, fax 843-792-5460 or 843-876-8055), creating potential confusion about which fax/email is correct.

### Patient Identification
Fields for: Patient Name, Date of Birth, Email Address, Phone #, Health Record #. No field for SSN (unlike form-2.pdf which asks for "Last 4 digits of SSN") ‚Äî this is a positive design choice for a dental-specific form.

### Release Direction
The form uses a combined "Release Records To / Obtain From" label with the parenthetical "(Where do you want the information sent? OR Who may receive the information?)" ‚Äî this is somewhat confusing as it conflates two directions (sending records out vs. receiving records in) in a single field. The main form (form-1) separates these into two distinct fields ("Release Records From" and "Release Records To").

### Record Types
The dental-specific record types include:
- Entire Medical Record
- Orthodontic treatment notes (Including orthodontic-related photos and x-rays)
- Radiology Images / DVD (NOT Included in Entire Record)
- Periodontic Charting
- Billing/Financial Statements

This is a reasonable set of dental-specific options not available on the general form.

### Purpose of Release
Options: Continuing Care, Disability, Legal, Patient Request, School, Military, Insurance, Other ‚Äî standard set matching the other MUSC forms.

### Release Method
Options: Fax (for healthcare providers/organizations as permitted), DVD/CD, Email, Mail, Other. Notably **missing**: MyChart and Encrypted Email options that appear on the newer form-1.

### Authorization Language
The legal disclosure section is substantial and covers:
- Sensitive information notice (psychiatric/psychological care, sexual assault, drug abuse, HIV/AIDS, alcohol abuse)
- Right to revoke in writing, directed to "Dental Health Information Services Department (Dental Records)"
- One-year expiration
- Voluntary nature of authorization
- Reference to 45 CFR ¬ß164.524
- Re-disclosure risk warning

The statement "This form must be COMPLETED in its entirety in order to be considered valid" appears in bold-style emphasis. While this is a common form convention, it's worth noting as potentially intimidating.

This address (MSC 507) differs from the address on form-1 for dental records (3 South Park Circle / Bldg. 3 / Suite 103), suggesting the department may have relocated between the 5/19 version date and the 10/2023 revision of form-1.

## 5. PATIENT-CENTEREDNESS

**Strengths:**
- Clear submission instructions (fax and email) at the top
- Dental-specific record categories are tailored to what dental patients actually need
- Does not require SSN
- States "I do not need to sign this form to receive treatment" ‚Äî reassuring
- Mentions the right to review/copy information under 45 CFR ¬ß164.524
- One-year expiration provides a reasonable time-limited scope

**Weaknesses:**
- Not fillable digitally ‚Äî requires printing, handwriting, and then faxing/emailing (scanning first)
- The "Release Records To / Obtain From" combined field is confusing
- "A copy of my identification will be made and attached to this authorization" ‚Äî this implies in-person presentation for ID copying, which is a barrier for remote submission. It's ambiguous whether emailing/faxing a copy of ID would suffice.
- Fee language is vague: "I understand that fees for copies of medical records/Images and postage fees may be charged as provided by S.C. Law" ‚Äî no fee schedule or estimate is provided
- Requires a witness signature, which is a burden for patients completing the form at home
- The 30-day processing note is stated parenthetically at the bottom: "(NOTE: HIPAA LAW ALLOWS 30 DAYS from receipt for processing.)" ‚Äî while factually accurate for third-party requests, this may mislead patients requesting their own records (HIPAA allows 30 days for patient access requests under ¬ß164.524, but this form doesn't clearly distinguish patient access from third-party release)

## 6. COMPLIANCE

- **Fees**: References "S.C. Law" for fee authority but provides no specifics. For patient access requests, HIPAA limits fees to a reasonable, cost-based fee. The form does not distinguish between patient access fees and third-party release fees.
- **Processing time**: States 30 days, which aligns with HIPAA's maximum for access requests. However, HIPAA requires this to be the outer bound, not the norm ‚Äî the form presents it as the standard expectation.
- **ID requirement**: "A copy of my identification will be made and attached to this authorization" ‚Äî HIPAA allows verification of identity but does not require a photo ID copy to be attached. This may exceed HIPAA's verification requirements.
- **Witness requirement**: The form includes a "Witness Signature" field. HIPAA does not require a witness for authorization forms. This adds friction, particularly for patients submitting remotely.
- **Revocation**: Properly notes the right to revoke in writing. However, directing revocation to the "Dental Health Information Services Department (Dental Records)" specifically could be interpreted as requiring in-person or mail delivery.
- **Sensitive information**: The blanket inclusion statement ("this information may include reference to psychiatric / psychological care, sexual assault, drug abuse, results of tests for all infectious diseases including HIV / AIDS and / or alcohol abuse") is appropriate for an authorization form, though 42 CFR Part 2 protections for substance use disorder records require separate consent ‚Äî this form does not address Part 2 with the specificity that form-1 does.
