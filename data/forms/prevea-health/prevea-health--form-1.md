## 1. DOCUMENT IDENTIFICATION

This is a single-page **Authorization for Disclosure of Health Information** form (form number PRV_839, rev. 0118). It is a general-purpose ROI form covering both patient self-requests and third-party disclosures. It is the only English-language authorization form found for Prevea Health; translations in Hmong, Somali, and Spanish were also available but are substantively the same document. No separate patient-access-specific form was identified.

The form references "HSHS" (Hospital Sisters Health System) in the email disclaimer section, indicating an affiliation or shared form template with that health system, despite Prevea Health being categorized as an independent physician practice.

## 4. CONTENT DESIGN

**Structure:** The form is logically organized into 9 numbered sections:
1. Patient Information (name, address, DOB, phone, previous names)
2. Authorizes (source provider)
3. To Disclose To (recipient, with delivery options)
4. Date range of information
5. Information to be disclosed (checkboxes for record types)
6. Expiration
7. Purpose
8. Patient rights
9. Signature

**Length:** Single page, which is efficient for the amount of content covered. However, fitting this much content onto one page results in dense text, particularly in section 8.

**Clarity:** Mixed. Sections 1-7 use relatively clear, structured formatting with checkboxes and labeled fields. Section 8 ("YOUR RIGHTS WITH RESPECT TO THIS AUTHORIZATION") is a single dense paragraph of legalese running approximately 200 words with complex nested clauses such as: *"I may revoke this Authorization at any time by notifying the authorizing provider's health information department, as listed above, in writing and will not be effective as to uses and/or disclosures already made in reliance upon this Authorization, needed for an insurer to contest a claim/policy as authorized by law if signing the Authorization was a condition to obtaining insurance coverage, or to submit a claim to third party payers as provided in this Authorization after having provided treatment in reliance upon this Authorization."* This sentence is effectively unparseable for a typical patient.

**Checkbox content in Section 5** is reasonably granular, offering: Abstract of record/Pertinent records, History & physical, Emergency Department report, Consultation reports, Radiology/Imaging reports, Laboratory/Pathology, Radiology/Imaging films/CD, Progress notes, Discharge summary, Operative reports, EKG, and Billing records ‚Äî plus a free-text field for specific records.

**Restriction options:** Section 5 also includes opt-out checkboxes: *"I DO NOT WANT THE FOLLOWING INFORMATION DISCLOSED: Alcohol/Drug Abuse, HIV Test Results, Mental Health/Developmental Disabilities"* ‚Äî allowing patients to restrict sensitive categories, which reflects Wisconsin and federal 42 CFR Part 2 requirements.

## 5. PATIENT-CENTEREDNESS

**Self-request support:** Section 3 explicitly includes a "Self" checkbox under "TO DISCLOSE TO," which is a positive signal that the form is intended for patient access, not only third-party releases. It is not, however, a *dedicated* patient-access form.

**Delivery options:** Notably robust. The form offers five delivery methods: Pick up, Mail to address above, View on-site, Electronic Format, and E-mail. The email option further distinguishes between encrypted and unencrypted email, with a risk acknowledgment for unencrypted delivery. This is above-average for ROI forms.

**Date range defaults:** Section 4 states *"If left blank, only information from the past two (2) years will be disclosed."* This is a restrictive default ‚Äî patients who want a complete record must affirmatively specify a date range. The form also states *"Future dates will not be honored,"* which is appropriate but limits ongoing authorization.

**Purpose options (Section 7):** Includes "Patient Request" as a checkbox option alongside Continuing Care, Legal Investigation/Action, Insurance Eligibility/Benefits, and Other. The note "copy fees may apply" is included parenthetically.

**Rights disclosure (Section 8):** The form does reference patient rights ‚Äî right to inspect/receive a copy, right to revoke, no obligation to sign, non-conditioning of treatment on signing. However, it does not cite HIPAA ¬ß 164.524 by name, and there is no mention of electronic health information (EHI) export or bulk data access under the 21st Century Cures Act.

**Expiration:** Section 6 states the authorization is *"good until the following date/event"* and defaults to 1 year if left blank. One year is a standard and reasonable default.

## 6. COMPLIANCE

**Fee mention:** The form notes *"copy fees may apply"* under Purpose but does not disclose specific fee amounts. This is neutral ‚Äî not a barrier, but not fully transparent.

**No notarization required.** The form requires only patient signature (or legal representative signature) and, for AODA/Mental Health disclosures, a witness signature ‚Äî consistent with legal requirements.

**Submission method:** The form does not specify how to submit (mail, fax, in-person). No in-person-only restriction is stated, but no fax number or mailing address for submission is provided on the form itself ‚Äî only the authorizing provider's fax is requested in Section 2.

**Photo ID requirement:** The form states for pickup: *"to pick up my records. (Photo ID required.)"* This is reasonable for in-person pickup.

**Legal representative provisions:** The form includes provisions for minors, legally incompetent/incapacitated persons, and deceased patients, with appropriate legal authority checkboxes (parent, legal guardian, activated POA for Health Care, next of kin/executor). The parental declaration *"I hereby declare that I have not been denied physical placement of this child"* reflects Wisconsin-specific custody law.

**42 CFR Part 2 / AODA:** The form includes a specific statement about federal regulation: *"Federal Regulation (42 CRF, Part 2)/AODA prohibits any further disclosure without specific written consent of the person to whom it pertains."* Note the typo: "CRF" should be "CFR."
