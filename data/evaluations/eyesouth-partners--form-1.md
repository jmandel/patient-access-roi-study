## 6. COMPLIANCE

**Expiration**: The form states "This Release of Information will remain in effect until terminated by me in writing." This is an indefinite authorization with no automatic expiration ‚Äî unusual and potentially problematic. HIPAA requires an expiration date or event for valid authorizations (45 CFR ¬ß 164.508(c)(1)(v)), though this requirement technically applies to authorizations under ¬ß 164.508, not necessarily to patient access requests under ¬ß 164.524.

**Fee disclosure**: No mention of fees. This is neutral ‚Äî neither good nor bad, but patients receive no information about potential costs.

**Submission method**: The form lists fax numbers for each location, implying fax submission is available. No explicit statement about accepted submission methods (mail, fax, in-person, email).

**Revocation**: Only "terminated by me in writing" ‚Äî no specific instructions on how to terminate, who to contact, or where to send the revocation.

**Missing elements**: No witness or notarization requirements (positive). No mention of representative authorization beyond the signature line labeled "Patient or Legal Representative."
