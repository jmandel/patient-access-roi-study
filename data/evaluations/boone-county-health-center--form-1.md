## 5. PATIENT-CENTEREDNESS

**Patient access vs. third-party release**: The form does **not** distinguish between a patient requesting their own records and a third-party authorization. The structure with "Release Records FROM" and "TO" fields treats all disclosures identically. There is no mention of HIPAA's Right of Access (45 CFR ¬ß 164.524), no reference to patient rights, and no acknowledgment that patients have a legal entitlement to their records.

**Scope options**: The form offers reasonable granularity for record selection ‚Äî 12 specific record type checkboxes (History & Physical, Progress Notes, Lab Reports, X-Ray Reports, ER Records, Consultation Reports, EKG/Cardiac Records, Immunization Record, Therapies, Billing Information, Discharge Plan, Discharge Report). Date-of-service options include "Current," "1 Year," and "2 Years," with a note: "If dates are not provided only the past year will be sent."

**Format options**: Limited to physical formats: "Paper copies [ ] Fax [ ] CD [ ] USB Memory Stick/ Thumb Drive [ ]." No electronic delivery option (email, portal, etc.). The newer form-2 adds encrypted email.

**Expiration**: The authorization states it "is effective for _____ months after the date it was signed," leaving the duration as a blank fill-in. This puts the burden on the patient (or staff) to determine an appropriate timeframe, with no default or guidance. The newer form-2 resolved this by setting a fixed 12-month expiration.

**EHI/bulk export**: No mention of electronic health information export, patient portals, or bulk data access.

## 6. COMPLIANCE

**Fee disclosure**: The form states "I realize I will be charged a copy fee of : 0:25 cents per page plus postage." Under HIPAA, for patient access requests, fees may only cover the cost of labor for copying, supplies, and postage. The $0.25 per page flat fee is within the HIPAA safe harbor of $0.10/page for paper or the "reasonable, cost-based fee" standard, though $0.25 may be at the high end. However, the form makes no distinction between patient-access fees and third-party disclosure fees ‚Äî the same fee appears to apply universally. For patient access requests specifically, HIPAA limits what can be charged, and a per-page fee for electronic copies is generally not permitted.

**Identification requirement**: The form includes "Verified with photo identification and copied" with an employee signature line, suggesting photo ID is required and photocopied. While identity verification is reasonable, photocopying IDs may create unnecessary friction and data-retention concerns.

**No submission instructions**: The form provides no guidance on how to submit ‚Äî no address for mailing, no fax number for form submission (though a general HIM fax is in the header), no email, no mention of in-person drop-off or portal upload.

**Revocation**: The form correctly notes the right to revoke authorization in writing, and that revocation won't apply to actions already taken.
