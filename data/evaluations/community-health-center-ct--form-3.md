## 6. COMPLIANCE

- **Reason for request**: The form asks patients to indicate why they are requesting records ("FIRMO ESTA AUTORIZACI√ìN POR EL SIGUIENTE MOTIVO"). Under HIPAA, patients exercising their right of access are not required to provide a reason. However, this form bundles release-to-third-party and self-request on the same form, and reason-for-request is more standard for third-party releases. Still, a patient using the self-request pathway may feel obligated to justify their request.
- **Sensitive information opt-in**: The separate consent required for drug/alcohol, psychiatric, and HIV/AIDS information aligns with 42 CFR Part 2 and state confidentiality laws. The form appropriately requires explicit checkboxes for each category.
- **Revocation rights**: Properly disclosed ‚Äî must be in writing to the Medical Records Department.
- **Voluntariness**: The form states "No es necesario que firme este formulario para garantizar mi tratamiento m√©dico," correctly advising that signing is not a condition of treatment.
- **Default 3-year scope**: The form defaults to releasing only the last 3 years of records unless the patient specifies otherwise. This could be a barrier for patients seeking their complete record, particularly if they don't read the fine print or don't specify a broader date range.
