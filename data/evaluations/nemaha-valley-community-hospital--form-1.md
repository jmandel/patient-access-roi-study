Both pages carry the dual-branded header with the Nemaha Valley Community Hospital and Seneca Family Practice logos, addresses, and phone numbers. ## 5. PATIENT-CENTEREDNESS

**Patient access vs. third-party release**: The form is a general-purpose release authorization. It asks the patient to authorize disclosure "to" a named recipient ‚Äî this is structured as a third-party release form, not as a patient access request under HIPAA ¬ß 164.524. There is no language distinguishing a patient requesting their own records from a patient authorizing release to an insurance company, attorney, or another provider. A patient wanting their own records would need to list themselves as the recipient.

**Scope options**: The form offers a checklist of 14 specific record types, which is a useful feature for patients who want targeted records. The form also includes a treatment date range field. However, there is no "all records" checkbox option.

**Format/delivery options**: The form itself offers no electronic delivery options. Page 2's cost calculation section mentions pick-up, mail, and fax as delivery methods ‚Äî but no email or patient portal option. The cost sheet references Memory Stick ($10) and CD ($10) as media options, which were current in 2014 but are now outdated.

**Right of Access language**: The form does not reference HIPAA ¬ß 164.524 or the patient's right of access. It does not explain that patients have a right to obtain their records. The form references the right to refuse signing and the right to revoke, but these are standard authorization elements, not access rights.

**EHI/electronic export**: No mention whatsoever.

**SSN requirement**: The form includes a field for "SOCIAL SECURITY NUMBER" as part of patient identification. While not explicitly marked as required, its prominent placement alongside name, birthdate, and MRN suggests it is expected. Collecting SSN for a records release is unnecessary and creates identity theft risk.

## 6. COMPLIANCE

**Fee structure**: The per-page rate of $0.03 is extremely low and well within HIPAA limits. However, the $3.90/15-minute labor charge (with a 15-minute minimum) is a flat labor fee that applies regardless of request size. For a patient requesting a small number of records under their right of access, HIPAA limits fees to a reasonable, cost-based amount ‚Äî a minimum $3.90 labor charge on top of per-page fees could be questioned for very small requests, though it is not egregious.

**Media fees**: $10 for a Memory Stick or CD is a media charge. Under HIPAA's right of access rules, if a patient requests electronic copies, the covered entity may charge only for labor ‚Äî the cost of portable media like a CD or USB drive can be included, but $10 may exceed the actual cost of a blank CD.

**Expiration**: The form includes a blank expiration field ("This authorization will expire on the following date or event:") and a footer note stating "Kansas SB 119 mandates that all authorizations are no longer valid after one year from the date of signature." This is a proper reference to Kansas state law.

**Revocation**: The form states the patient can revoke in writing and directs them to "MEDICAL RECORDS AT THE ABOVE PHONE NUMBERS." This is compliant.

**No notarization or witness requirement**: The form requires only the patient/representative signature, with no notary, witness, or in-person submission requirement explicitly stated. However, the lack of any submission instructions (no mailing address, no fax number, no email for form submission) means patients may not know how to submit the completed form without calling.

**Both pages labeled "Page 2 of 2"**: Both pages of the form are marked "Page 2 of 2" in the footer, which is a formatting error that suggests the original page 1 may have been a different document, or this is a pagination mistake from the original form design.
