## 1. DOCUMENT IDENTIFICATION

Form-3 is an "Authorization to Disclose PHI (Protected Health Information)" ‚Äî a general-purpose ROI authorization form. The existence of two slightly different versions of the same form on different pages of the same website is notable and creates potential confusion for patients.

## 5. PATIENT-CENTEREDNESS

**Patient access vs. third-party release:** The form includes a "Self (same info as above)" checkbox in Section 2, explicitly supporting patients requesting their own records. This is a positive feature. However, the form is fundamentally a general-purpose disclosure authorization ‚Äî it's the same form whether a patient wants their own records or a third party wants them.

**Purpose options:** Section 4 offers "Personal," "Legal," "Insurance," "Continuation of Care," and "Other" ‚Äî a patient requesting their own records would select "Personal."

**Scope options:** Excellent granularity. Patients can request specific record types with date ranges (Section 5), choose from many specific categories, or request their entire record. The "Entire Record From: (date or date range)" option is clearly available.

**Format and delivery options:** Section 6 offers four format options (CD ‚Äî hospital only, Flash Drive, Encrypted Email, Paper) and three delivery methods (Fax, Mail, In-Person Pick Up). The inclusion of **encrypted email** as a delivery format is notable and patient-friendly. However, CD and Flash Drive are somewhat dated media choices.

**Right of Access language:** The form does not explicitly reference HIPAA ¬ß 164.524 or the patient's right of access. It states: *"I understand that I may refuse to sign this Authorization. If I do not sign this Authorization, Fisher-Titus Health will continue to provide treatment and seek payment for services provided."* This addresses non-retaliation but doesn't educate patients about their legal right to access their records.

**EHI Export / bulk data:** No mention of electronic health information export, USCDI, or any patient portal integration.

## 6. COMPLIANCE

**Fees:** The form states: *"Fisher-Titus Health may charge a fee for providing the information specified above."* No fee schedule or amounts are disclosed on the form. The use of "may" is vague.

**Expiration:** Section 8 states the authorization "will automatically expire one year from the date signed below unless revoked or another date or event is written here." A 1-year expiration is standard and reasonable. The option to specify a different date or triggering event is a good flexibility feature.

**Revocation:** The form clearly states: *"I understand that I may revoke this Authorization in writing at any time, except to the extent that action has already been taken in response to the Authorization."* This is standard HIPAA-compliant language.

**Re-disclosure warning:** Present: *"I understand that the information disclosed pursuant to this Authorization may be subject to re-disclosure by the recipient and may no longer be protected under federal privacy law."*

**Treatment conditioning:** Appropriately addressed: *"If I do not sign this Authorization, Fisher-Titus Health will continue to provide treatment and seek payment for services provided."*

**Witness requirement:** The form includes a "Witness Signature" line with a date field. While not labeled as required, the presence of a witness line could be interpreted as a requirement, which HIPAA does not mandate for patient-requested access.

**SSN collection:** The form requests "SS#" (Social Security Number) in Section 1. While sometimes used for patient identification, collecting SSN on a form that may be faxed or mailed raises privacy concerns and is not required by HIPAA.
