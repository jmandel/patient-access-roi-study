## 4. CONTENT DESIGN

**Length and proportionality:** The form is a single page, which is appropriately concise for a patient access request. It covers identification, scope, format, and delivery preferences without excessive verbiage.

**Clarity:** The language is relatively plain. The opening instruction ‚Äî "If you would like a copy of your medical record, please complete the form below" ‚Äî is clear and patient-friendly. The form avoids dense legal language, with only a brief note about email risks ("you acknowledge and accept the risks outlined in Guidelines for E-mail with Patients, provided separately if applicable").

**Organization:** The form follows a logical flow:
1. Patient identification (name, DOB, address, SSN last 4, phone, email)
2. Purpose (copy for self vs. send to third party)
3. Date range
4. Record type selection (Hospital, Office/Clinic, Behavioral Health sections with checkboxes)
5. Format preference (CD, Flash Drive, E-mail, Paper, Other)
6. Delivery method (Mail, Secure e-mail, Fax, Pickup)
7. Signature and date

**Layout:** The form is densely packed for a single page. Three columns of checkboxes (Hospital, Office/Clinic, Behavioral Health) are arranged side by side, which maximizes space efficiency but creates a somewhat cramped appearance. The underline blanks are standard but make the form look dated.

**Readability:** Text appears to be in a reasonable font size (likely 9-10pt given the density). The form code "MEDR 015" and date "6/2016" appear at the bottom.

## 5. PATIENT-CENTEREDNESS

**Patient access focus:** This is explicitly a **Patient Request for Access** form, which is a positive distinction from the general third-party release form (form-2.pdf). The opening text and title clearly frame this as the patient requesting their own records. However, it also includes an option to "send my records to" a third party, making it a dual-purpose form.

**Scope options:** Excellent granularity. Patients can specify:
- Date range for records
- Specific record types across three categories: Hospital (7 options + Other), Office/Clinic (6 options + Other + Entire Record + Itemized Bill), and Behavioral Health (6 options + Other + Entire Record + Itemized Bill)

**Format options:** Notably good. The form offers five format choices: **CD, Flash Drive, E-mail, Paper copy, Other**. The inclusion of electronic delivery options (email, CD, flash drive) is above average for ROI forms.

**Delivery method options:** Four delivery methods are offered: **Mail, Secure e-mail, Fax, Pickup**. The "secure e-mail" option is a thoughtful addition.

**Right of Access language:** The form does not reference HIPAA ¬ß 164.524 or explicitly state the patient's right of access. It also does not mention any timeframe requirements (though a note says "it may take up to 30 days to schedule the appointment or provide copies").

**EHI Export:** No mention of electronic health information export or bulk data access.

**Personal representative:** The form includes a note that "If the patient lacks legal capacity or is unable to sign, an authorized personal representative may sign this for the patient. (Written Proof May be Requested)."

## 6. COMPLIANCE

**Fees:** No fees are mentioned or disclosed on this form. (By contrast, form-2.pdf mentions "A fee may be charged for providing the protected health information.")

**Expiration:** No expiration date is stated on this form.

**Barriers:** The form does not require notarization. No explicit requirement for in-person submission. However, the lack of fillable fields effectively requires printing and either hand-delivery, mailing, or faxing.

**SSN collection:** The form requests "Last 4 numbers of SSN" ‚Äî not the full SSN, which is reasonable for patient identification, though some patients may be uncomfortable providing even partial SSN on a form they must print and potentially mail or fax.

**30-day processing note:** The form states "it may take up to 30 days to schedule the appointment or provide copies," which aligns with HIPAA's 30-day response requirement.

**Wrong facility:** The most significant compliance concern is that this form is for St. Luke's Hospital, not AdventHealth Hendersonville. A Hendersonville patient using this form would be submitting a request to the wrong facility.
