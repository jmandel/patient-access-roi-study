## 4. CONTENT DESIGN

**Length**: One page ‚Äî appropriate for an authorization form. All content fits on a single page, which is commendable.

**Organization**: The form follows a logical flow: patient identification ‚Üí direction of disclosure (release vs. obtain) ‚Üí self-request option ‚Üí record types ‚Üí date range ‚Üí reason ‚Üí expiration ‚Üí legal notices ‚Üí signature. This is a sound structure.

**Clarity**: The language is mostly clear. The legal paragraph at the bottom is dense but covers required elements (right to revoke, voluntary nature, redisclosure warning). The sentence "I need not sign this form to ensure healthcare treatment" is a good plain-language inclusion.

**Layout concerns**: The pre-filled "OBTAIN" section creates visual confusion. In the extracted text, the recipient's name, address, city, state, zip, phone, and fax are scattered across multiple lines mixed with blank field markers. While this may render adequately in the PDF visually, the underlying text structure is messy, which would affect screen readers and text extraction.

**Record type options**: The form offers 9 checkbox categories including "Progress notes," "Consultation notes," "Complete health record (No telephone encounters)," "Complete health record (With telephone encounters)," "Dental records, including x-rays," "Labs," "Immunizations," "X-ray, CT Scan, MRI, US results," and the B23-specific "B23 Site/Evaluations/Assesments/IFSPs." There is also an "Other" free-text field.

**Spelling error**: "Assesments" is misspelled (should be "Assessments") in the B23-specific checklist item.

## 5. PATIENT-CENTEREDNESS

**Patient access vs. third-party release**: The form serves dual purposes ‚Äî both releasing records to a third party and obtaining records from a third party. It also includes a self-request option: "If releasing information to ME, my medical records should be released via: Mail / Fax / Pick Up / E-Mail." This is good ‚Äî patients can use this form to request their own records.

**Delivery options**: Four delivery methods are offered for self-requests: mail, fax, pick-up, and email. The inclusion of email is a positive feature, providing a digital delivery channel.

**Scope control**: Patients can specify date ranges ("From the dates ___ to ___", "From start of care to present", "Last three (3) years"), specific record types via checkboxes, and can exclude specific drug/alcohol information. This gives patients meaningful control over what is disclosed.

**Sensitive information protections**: Drug/alcohol abuse, psychiatric/behavioral health, and HIV/AIDS information require separate, explicit consent checkboxes ‚Äî consistent with 42 CFR Part 2 and state-level protections. The form also allows patients to specify exclusions for drug/alcohol information.

**Right of Access language**: The form does not explicitly reference HIPAA ¬ß 164.524 or use the phrase "Right of Access." It does state "I understand that I have a legal right to revoke this authorization at any time" and notes the authorization is voluntary.

**EHI/bulk export**: No mention of electronic health information export, USCDI, or bulk data access.

**Processing time**: The form states "CHC has 30 days to fulfill my request" ‚Äî this is within the HIPAA-permitted timeframe (30 days, extendable to 60).

**Purpose**: However, as a program-specific form pre-configured for Birth to Three referrals, this document is not well-suited for a patient simply trying to access their own records. A patient would need to find form-2 (the generic version) or navigate to the Luma Health portal for general record requests.

## 6. COMPLIANCE

**Expiration**: The form states "This authorization will expire 90 days from the date on which it was signed, unless I indicate a different expiration event or date below." The pre-filled value is "Third Birthday." For the Birth to Three context, this makes sense (the program serves children age 0-3), but 90 days as a default is relatively short compared to many organizations. This is not a compliance concern ‚Äî shorter expirations protect patients ‚Äî but patients need to be aware if they want a longer window.

**No unreasonable barriers**: The form does not require notarization, witnesses, or in-person submission. It provides a fax number (860-343-7379) and mailing address for submission.

**Fee disclosure**: No fees are mentioned on the form.

**Typo in legal text**: The form contains "in response to his authorization" ‚Äî should read "this authorization." This same typo appears in form-2 as well, indicating it was inherited from the template.

**Bundling**: The form does not bundle records release with other authorizations. It is single-purpose.
