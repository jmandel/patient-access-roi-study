## 5. PATIENT-CENTEREDNESS

**Not a patient access form.** This form does not serve a patient trying to access their own health records. It serves the opposite purpose ‚Äî authorizing the organization to use the patient's information for media, marketing, and educational purposes. A patient should never need to fill out this form to obtain their records.

**Scope options:** Limited to three categories (media, marketing, educational) plus an "other" option. For information type, the options are "Nature of injury or illness," "Nature of treatment," and "Recordings" (defined as "Any and all recordings, including, but not limited to videotapes, photographs, or electronic, digital or audio recordings"). This is appropriately narrow for a marketing release.

**Sensitive information consent:** The form separately addresses drug/alcohol use, psychiatric, HIV/AIDS, Hepatitis B or C, and STD information with a Yes/No checkbox, which is appropriate ‚Äî patients should have explicit control over whether sensitive categories are included in marketing materials.

**Format/delivery options:** None mentioned. The form does not address how the authorized disclosure will occur, which is reasonable since the organization (not the patient) would be initiating the use.

**Voluntariness statement:** Clearly stated upfront: "If I refuse to participate, my refusal will not affect my ability or that of my dependent(s) to receive health care from Harris Health System." This is a good practice.
