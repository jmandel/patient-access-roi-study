## 1. DOCUMENT IDENTIFICATION

Form-4.pdf is the **current English-language version** (dated 09/2025) of Blessing Health System's general authorization for release of medical information. It is a 2-page document: page 1 is the authorization form itself, page 2 provides line-by-line "Guidelines for Completing" the form.

Five forms were retrieved for this organization:

- **form-1.pdf** and **form-2.pdf**: April 2023 English versions (nearly identical; form-1 has a "Medical Record Number" header, form-2 does not). Both include a **witness signature requirement** that form-4 removes.
- **form-3.pdf**: An older undated version (revised 8/2015) with a different layout listing specific Blessing facilities (Blessing Hospital, Blessing Physician Services, Blessing Walk-In, Illini Community Hospital) and separating record types into "Hospital Setting" and "Office Setting" categories.
- **form-4.pdf** (this document): The current September 2025 English version, linked from the website's Medical Records page.
- **form-5.pdf**: The September 2025 Spanish translation of this form, also currently linked from the website.

Form-4 represents the most recent iteration, with a notable improvement: the removal of the witness signature requirement that appeared in the April 2023 versions. The form is managed through PolicyStat (official copy at https://blessing-forms.policystat.com/), indicating centralized document control.

## 4. CONTENT DESIGN

**Length and proportionality:** 2 pages is reasonable ‚Äî page 1 for the authorization, page 2 for completion guidelines. The guidelines page is a genuinely useful addition that walks through each field.

**Structure and organization:** The form follows a logical flow: patient demographics ‚Üí release from/to ‚Üí record types ‚Üí sensitive information consent ‚Üí purpose ‚Üí revocation rights ‚Üí expiration ‚Üí signature. Sections are visually delineated with bold headers ("RELEASE RECORDS FROM:", "RECORDS GOING TO:", "Sensitive Medical Information").

**Record type granularity:** The form offers 15 specific record type checkboxes plus an "Other" field:
- Continuing Care Abstract, Discharge Summary, Radiology Reports, Immunization Records, History and Physical, Radiology Images, Operative Reports, Consultations, Therapy Notes, Physical Forms, Emergency Department Records, Cardiology Reports, Workability or School Release Forms, Laboratory and Pathology Reports, Office Notes.

This is a good level of specificity, though the descriptions are clinical rather than plain-language.

**Language clarity:** The form uses a mix of plain language and legal phrasing. The authorization statement ("I authorize the use or disclosure of the above named individual's health information, its employees and agents, to furnish") is grammatically awkward ‚Äî "its employees and agents" appears to modify "health information" rather than the organization, creating ambiguity about who is authorized. The re-disclosure warning is dense but substantively complete.

**Guidelines page:** The page 2 guidelines are written in plain, helpful language. Entries like "Date of records needed, this can be a date range (i.e. '1999 to present', or specific lab report on 06/01/12.)" provide concrete examples. The "Expiration" guidance is clear: "Any date can be written here. If left blank, 6 months will apply."

## 5. PATIENT-CENTEREDNESS

**Patient access vs. third-party release:** This is a **general-purpose authorization form**, not specifically designed for patient access to their own records. The bidirectional "RELEASE RECORDS FROM" / "RECORDS GOING TO" structure is oriented toward third-party disclosures. However, the purpose checkboxes include "Personal reasons" and "Transferring care," which at least accommodate patient-initiated requests.

**Delivery options:** The form offers four delivery methods: "Pick up or Mail or Fax or Email." Email is included, which is better than many ROI forms. However, there is no mention of secure electronic delivery, patient portal access, or EHI export options. The form warns that "Electronic images/records (i.e. Radiology) on CD/USB media are not encrypted or password-protected" and that "Unencrypted CD/USB media cannot be mailed by Blessing," which suggests physical media delivery exists but is constrained.

**Scope options:** Patients can specify date ranges (marked "MUST specify date range"), select from 15 record type categories, and separately authorize sensitive information categories (Mental Health, Genetic Testing, STD/AIDS/HIV, Drugs/Alcohol). This is granular and allows tailored requests.

**HIPAA Right of Access:** The form does **not** reference HIPAA ¬ß 164.524 or explicitly state the patient's right of access. It mentions that authorization is voluntary and that treatment/payment are not conditioned on signing, which addresses HIPAA's anti-conditioning requirement ‚Äî but it does not educate patients about their specific right to obtain their own records.

**EHI Export / bulk data:** No mention.

**Fees:** The form itself does not disclose fees. The retrieval notes mention a $6.50 flat fee for certain record requests, but this is not stated on the form.

**Photo ID:** The form states "I understand photo identification may be required to obtain medical records" ‚Äî the hedged "may be required" language is less burdensome than a strict mandate but still ambiguous about when ID would be needed.

## 6. COMPLIANCE

**Witness requirement removed:** The September 2025 version notably removes the "Witness signature is required below" requirement that existed in the April 2023 versions (form-1 and form-2). This is a positive change ‚Äî witness requirements for routine record requests exceed HIPAA requirements and create unnecessary barriers.

**Expiration:** Default of 6 months if not specified. "This Authorization is only valid for existing records. Future services will need a new Authorization." The 6-month default is reasonable and standard.

**Conditioning:** The form correctly states: "Healthcare treatment, payment, enrollment in the health plan, or eligibility for benefits is not conditioned on signing the Authorization."

**Revocation rights:** Clearly stated: "I understand I have the right to revoke this Authorization at any time. I understand if I revoke this Authorization, I must do so in writing and present my written revocation to the Health Information Management Department."

**Sensitive information handling:** Separate authorization for mental health, genetic testing, STD/AIDS/HIV, and drugs/alcohol records ‚Äî consistent with Illinois state law requirements (Mental Health and Developmental Disabilities Confidentiality Act). Minors age 12+ in Illinois must sign for sensitive information release, which is correctly noted.

**No notarization required.** No in-person-only submission requirement ‚Äî multiple submission channels implied by the contact information (mailing addresses, fax numbers, phone numbers for three locations).
