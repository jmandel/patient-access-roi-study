## 4. CONTENT DESIGN

**The form is concise but dense, cramming substantial content onto a single page.**

**Structure:** The form follows a logical top-to-bottom flow:
1. Recipient information ("To Recipient")
2. Source/patient information ("From Clinic/Hospital" / "Patient")
3. Dates of service selection
4. Records type selection
5. Purpose for disclosure
6. HIPAA acknowledgment checkboxes
7. Signature block
8. Submission instructions

**Language quality:** The form mixes plain-language instructions with HIPAA regulatory citations. The acknowledgment section is notably dense, with sentences like: "I understand that my records are confidential and cannot be disclosed without my written authorization except when otherwise permitted by law. Information used or disclosed pursuant to this authorization may be subject to redisclosure by the recipient and no longer protected." This is standard legal language, but not particularly patient-friendly.

The form **cites specific CFR sections inline** ‚Äî e.g., "(45 CFR ¬ß 164.508(c)(1)(i))" after "Records to be Released" and "(45 CFR ¬ß 164.508(c)(2)(i))" after the revocation acknowledgment. While legally thorough, this is not how patients naturally read or understand their rights.

**Scope of records options:** The form provides 11 record type checkboxes: All Medical Records (no films), History & Physical, Consultation Reports, Emergency Room Record, Operative Report, Discharge Summary, Lab/Pathology Reports, Radiology Reports, Images (check for CD of films), Itemized Billing, and Other. This is a reasonably comprehensive selection.

**Purpose options:** Six purpose checkboxes: Disability, Insurance, Attorney, Referring Physician, Patient Request, Other. The inclusion of "Patient Request" is appropriate for patient-access scenarios.

**Layout:** Everything fits on one page, which is efficient but results in tight spacing. The form uses horizontal lines for fill-in fields and "o" characters for checkboxes, giving it a somewhat dated, typewriter-era appearance.

## 5. PATIENT-CENTEREDNESS

**This form is a general-purpose release authorization, not designed specifically for patient access requests.**

- **Patient access vs. third-party release:** The form's structure is oriented toward third-party disclosure. The opening line reads: "Upon presentation of this authorization you are requested to provide the records outlined below to: To Recipient:" ‚Äî implying records are being released to a third party. A patient requesting their own records would need to list themselves as the "Recipient," which, while possible, is not intuitive. "Patient Request" is listed as one of six purpose options, but it's not the default or primary use case.
- **"From Clinic/Hospital" field:** This is a generic field ‚Äî the patient must fill in which facility they're requesting records from. This makes sense for a vendor like HealthMark that serves many facilities, but a HopeHealth-branded form would have this pre-filled.
- **Format/delivery options:** The form provides **no options for delivery format**. There is no checkbox for electronic delivery, email, CD, portal access, or mail. The form doesn't specify how the patient will receive their records at all.
- **Date range options:** The form offers two date-of-service options ‚Äî complete file for all dates, or a specified date range ‚Äî which gives patients reasonable control.
- **HIPAA Right of Access:** The form does not explicitly mention the patient's right of access under 45 CFR ¬ß 164.524. The HIPAA citations present (¬ß 164.508) relate to the authorization requirements for disclosure, not to the patient access right. This is an important distinction ‚Äî ¬ß 164.508 governs authorizations for uses and disclosures, while ¬ß 164.524 governs the patient's right to access their own records.
- **EHI Export:** No mention of electronic health information export, TEFCA, or any modern interoperability concepts.

## 6. COMPLIANCE

- **Expiration:** The authorization expires "One Hundred Eighty (180) days from the date of my signature." This is a fixed, non-patient-configurable expiration. 180 days (approximately 6 months) is a moderate timeframe ‚Äî not unreasonably short, but the patient cannot extend it or set their own expiration.
- **Revocation:** The form includes a standard revocation acknowledgment: "I may revoke this authorization in writing at any time except to the extent that action has been taken in reliance upon this authorization." This is HIPAA-compliant language.
- **Conditioning of treatment:** The form includes a correct statement that "treatment or payment cannot be conditioned on my signing this authorization" with appropriate exceptions noted (research, pre-employment testing). This aligns with 45 CFR ¬ß 164.508(c)(2)(ii).
- **Sensitive information disclosure:** The form includes a blanket acknowledgment regarding "history, diagnosis, and/or treatment of drug or alcohol abuse, mental illness, or communicable disease, including Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) and Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS)." This is appropriate HIPAA language but may not satisfy stricter state-level consent requirements (e.g., 42 CFR Part 2 for substance use disorder records, which historically required separate consent).
- **Submission method:** The form directs submission to "HealthMark Group: Requestor Support Team" via email (status@healthmark-group.com) or phone ((800) 659-4035 Ext. 2). No mention of fax, mail, or in-person submission alternatives. Email submission of a signed form is convenient but raises questions about how signature authentication works in practice.
- **Fees:** No fee information is disclosed on the form.
- **No notarization or witness requirement** ‚Äî the form requires only the patient's (or authorized representative's) signature and printed name.
