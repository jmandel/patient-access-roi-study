## PATIENT-CENTEREDNESS

**This document is not an ROI form and cannot be used to request records.** As an NPP, it informs patients of their rights but provides no mechanism to exercise them.

The document does mention the right to access records: "You can ask to see or get an electronic or paper copy of your medical record and other health information we have about you. Ask us how to do this." However, the instruction is simply "Ask us how to do this" ‚Äî no phone number, no URL, no form reference, and no mention of the Sharecare portal that Gastro Florida actually uses for records requests.

The NPP states: "We will provide a copy or a summary of your health information, usually within 30 days of your request. We may charge a reasonable, cost-based fee." This language is consistent with HIPAA requirements.

**No submission pathway described:** The document does not tell patients how to initiate a records request. A patient reading this would know they have the right but not how to exercise it.

**Complaint process:** The document provides a complaint process: "please ask our staff for a Privacy Complaint Form" and gives the HHS OCR address, phone number, and URL. This is adequate.

## COMPLIANCE

As an informational notice rather than a request form, compliance concerns are limited. Notable observations:

- **Fee language:** "We may charge a reasonable, cost-based fee" ‚Äî consistent with HIPAA's permissible fee structure.
- **Timeline:** "usually within 30 days" ‚Äî consistent with the 30-day HIPAA requirement (with possible 30-day extension).
- **Summary option:** "We will provide a copy or a summary of your health information" ‚Äî under HIPAA, a summary may only be provided if the patient agrees to it. The phrasing here implies the practice can choose to provide a summary rather than a full copy, which could be misleading.
- **Retaliation:** "We will not retaliate against you for filing a complaint" ‚Äî appropriate inclusion.
