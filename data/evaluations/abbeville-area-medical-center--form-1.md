## COMPLIANCE

**Expiration**: The authorization expires after 90 days: "this authorization will expire ninety (90) days from the date it is signed." This is a relatively short window. While not per se illegal, it places time pressure on the organization to process the request and could require patients to resubmit if processing is delayed.

**Revocation**: Revocation must be "in writing" ‚Äî standard and compliant.

**Sensitive information**: The form includes blanket consent for sensitive categories: "This includes specific permission to release medical information pertaining to psychological, psychiatric or other mental impairment(s), drug abuse, alcoholism, or other substance abuse, sickle cell anemia, HIV infection (including AIDS or tests for HIV) or sexually transmitted diseases." This is bundled into the main authorization rather than offered as a separate opt-in, meaning patients cannot easily exclude sensitive records from their release.

**Conditioning treatment**: The form correctly states: "I understand that any treatment I may receive may not be conditioned on whether I sign this authorization."

**Fees**: No fee schedule or cost information is disclosed on the form.

**Submission method**: No instructions on how or where to submit the completed form (no address, fax number, email, or drop-off location on the form itself).
