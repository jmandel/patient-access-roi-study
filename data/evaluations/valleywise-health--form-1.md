## 1. DOCUMENT IDENTIFICATION

This is a **general authorization form for disclosing PHI to a third party** (Form #43439, document code DT7787, revised August 2024). It is a 2-page bilingual document ‚Äî page 1 in English, page 2 is a full Spanish translation. The form is structured around 8 numbered sections covering patient information, recipient designation, information scope, sensitive-category exclusions, delivery method, verbal communication authorization, purpose of disclosure, and patient rights.

Critically, this is **not** a patient-access-specific form. It is designed for authorizing Valleywise Health to *disclose* records to a named third-party recipient. While "Personal Use" appears as one of the purpose checkboxes in section 7.0, the form is fundamentally structured around disclosure to a "Designated Recipient or Facility" (section 2.0), not around a patient exercising their HIPAA Right of Access.

Valleywise Health maintains a **separate** form specifically for patient access: Form 45643 ("Patient Request for Access to Protected Health Information," form-2.pdf). That form is explicitly designed for patients requesting their own records and cross-references form 43439 for sensitive-information authorizations. The existence of both forms means a patient requesting their own records should ideally use form-2, not this one.

## 4. CONTENT DESIGN

**Structure**: The form is well-organized with 8 clearly numbered sections (1.0 through 8.0), each with a descriptive header. The logical flow is sensible: patient info ‚Üí recipient ‚Üí information scope ‚Üí exclusions ‚Üí delivery ‚Üí verbal authorization ‚Üí purpose ‚Üí patient rights. This is a standard and effective ordering.

**Length**: 2 pages total, but since page 2 is the Spanish translation, the English content occupies a single page. This is compact for the amount of information covered and avoids unnecessary padding.

**Clarity**: Language is generally clear, though some sections mix plain language with legal phrasing. The patient rights section (8.0) is dense but covers required HIPAA disclosures comprehensively. The fee notice is indirect: "There may be a fee associated with your request for records; for more information, visit our website at www.valleywisehealth.org" ‚Äî this neither specifies amounts nor provides a direct link to fee information.

**Layout concerns**: The top of the form includes a "VALLEYWISE HEALTH INTERNAL USE ONLY" section with internal routing checkboxes ("Release of sensitive information during an encounter," "Release to be processed by HIM," "No additional action needed, scan to patients chart"). This internal-use block is visible to patients and occupies prominent real estate at the top of the form, which may be confusing. The section 4.0 exclusions require patients to initial each category, and section 6.0 verbal authorization requires initials ‚Äî adding friction.

**Bilingual presentation**: The full Spanish translation on page 2 is a positive feature, making the form accessible to Spanish-speaking patients without requiring a separate document.

## 5. PATIENT-CENTEREDNESS

**Primary concern**: This form is **not designed for patient access**. It is a third-party disclosure authorization. A patient requesting their own records under HIPAA's Right of Access (45 CFR ¬ß 164.524) should use form-2 instead. The inclusion of "Personal Use" as a purpose checkbox in section 7.0 creates ambiguity ‚Äî a patient might use this form for self-access, but the form's structure (requiring a "Designated Recipient or Facility" in section 2.0) doesn't align well with that use case.

**Scope options**: Section 3.0 provides reasonable scope choices: "Pertinent Information," "Entire Chart," "Specific Information," radiology reports/images, billing, and an open "Other" field. Date/year specification is available for each option. This is adequate but less granular than form-2's record-type checkboxes (which list H&P, discharge summary, nursing notes, operative reports, etc.).

**Sensitive information handling**: Section 4.0 uses an **opt-out exclusion model** for sensitive categories (HIV/AIDS, behavioral health, substance abuse, genetic testing). The heading reads: "I specifically Request Valleywise Health to EXCLUDE from Disclosure Protected Health Information Related to." This means sensitive information is **included by default** unless the patient affirmatively initials to exclude each category. This is the reverse of the more protective opt-in approach typically seen, where sensitive categories require explicit authorization to *include*. For a third-party disclosure form, this default-include approach for sensitive categories is concerning.

**Delivery options**: Section 5.0 offers a wide range: mail, MyChart, pick-up, CD, USB drive, email, fax, and paper. The email option includes a thoughtful notice about encryption and gives patients the choice to waive encryption with initials. This is a strong set of options.

**Right of Access language**: No reference to HIPAA ¬ß 164.524 or the Right of Access. Section 8.0 states authorization is voluntary and treatment won't be conditioned on signing, which is appropriate for a HIPAA authorization but does not address patient access rights.

**EHI Export**: No mention of electronic health information export or bulk data access.

## 6. COMPLIANCE

**Fee disclosure**: The form states "There may be a fee associated with your request for records; for more information, visit our website." Fees are neither specified nor capped on the form itself. This indirect reference gives no information about whether fees comply with HIPAA's cost-based limits for patient access requests.

**Authorization expiration**: "Unless otherwise specified or revoked in writing, this Authorization will expire one (1) year after the date of signature." This is a reasonable default and standard practice.

**Voluntary nature**: The form clearly states: "Treatment, payment, enrollment, or eligibility for benefits may not be conditioned on signing this authorization. The provider will not deny me treatment if I do not wish to sign this form." This is appropriate HIPAA language.

**Revocation rights**: Clearly explained ‚Äî revocation must be in writing, takes effect upon receipt, and doesn't apply to already-released information.

**Liability waiver**: The final bullet in section 8.0 states the patient "authorize[s] Valleywise Health, its employees, agents, officers, directors and medical staff members from any legal responsibility or liability for the disclosure of the above information." This is a broad liability release bundled into the authorization.

**No notarization or witness requirements**: The form requires only the patient's (or legal representative's) signature and date.
