## 1. DOCUMENT IDENTIFICATION

**form-1.pdf is not a Release of Information form.** It is a "JOINT NOTICE OF PRIVACY PRACTICES" ‚Äî a standard HIPAA-mandated Notice of Privacy Practices (NPP). The document opens with: *"JOINT NOTICE OF PRIVACY PRACTICES ‚Äî Your Rights. Our Responsibilities. This notice describes how medical information about you may be used and disclosed and how you can get access to this information."*

This is the wrong document type for the purposes of this study. An NPP is a required disclosure document that informs patients of their rights; it is not an authorization or request form that a patient would fill out to obtain their records.

The second document retrieved (form-2.pdf) is an "Employer Authorization form" for occupational health services (DOT physicals, drug testing, etc.) ‚Äî also not a patient-facing ROI form.

**No actual ROI/authorization form was found on kdmc.org.** The retrieval notes document an exhaustive search: multiple web queries, full site navigation, and a complete inventory of all PDFs hosted on the domain. The organization handles medical records requests exclusively through a third-party vendor, Datavant (formerly Swellbox), via an online wizard at `https://www.swellbox.com/kings-daughters-mc-wizard.html`. There is no downloadable PDF authorization form available to patients.

## 5. PATIENT-CENTEREDNESS

**This document fails the patient-centeredness test for records access because it is not an ROI form.** It does mention the right of access: *"You can ask to see or get an electronic or paper copy of your medical record and other health information we have about you. Ask us how to do this."* However, the instruction is vague ‚Äî "Ask us how to do this" provides no concrete next step, no link to the Datavant wizard, no phone number for HIM, and no mention of the online request process.

The NPP does reference several patient rights:
- Right to obtain copies of medical records ("usually within 30 days")
- Right to request corrections ("within 60 days")
- Right to request confidential communications
- Right to request restrictions on use/sharing
- Right to an accounting of disclosures
- Right to file complaints (Privacy Officer at 601-835-9335; HHS OCR contact info provided)

**What's missing:** The NPP does not reference the Datavant/Swellbox online portal, does not explain how to actually initiate a records request, does not mention fees, and does not link to any ROI form. A patient reading this document would know they *have* the right to access records but would not know *how* to exercise that right ‚Äî a critical gap.

**No mention of:** Electronic delivery options, EHI export, or HIPAA ¬ß 164.524 by citation.

## 6. COMPLIANCE

**The NPP itself poses no compliance issues** ‚Äî it is a standard, well-constructed notice. However, the broader records access picture raises questions:

- **Third-party vendor model:** KDMC has outsourced medical records fulfillment entirely to Datavant. While this is common and not inherently problematic, the **fee structure ($6.50 flat + $0.12/page)** disclosed in the retrieval notes may warrant scrutiny. Under HIPAA's Right of Access (45 CFR ¬ß 164.524), fees for patient access must be limited to reasonable, cost-based fees for labor of copying. The per-page charge model is a legacy approach that has drawn OCR enforcement attention.
- **No downloadable form:** The absence of any downloadable authorization form means patients must use the Datavant online wizard. While an online process can be more convenient, it also means patients who lack internet access or who prefer paper have no self-service option discoverable on the website.
- **Photo ID for pickup:** The notes mention photo ID is required, which is a standard identity verification practice.
