**No mention of HIPAA Right of Access**: The form does not reference 45 CFR ¬ß 164.524 or explain the patient's right to access their own records. There is no acknowledgment that patient self-access requests have different legal requirements than third-party releases.

"Patient's email" appears as a field label but is positioned under the recipient section, making its purpose ambiguous.

**Processing time**: The instructions state "Please allow 10 business days for processing," which is within the 30-day HIPAA limit (though HIPAA allows up to 30 calendar days with a possible 30-day extension).

**Fee disclosure**: The form states "a fee may be charged for copies of medical records" and directs billing questions to CIOX Health at 1-800-367-1500. For patient self-access requests, HIPAA limits fees to a reasonable cost-based amount ‚Äî using a third-party ROI vendor for patient access requests raises concerns about fee transparency.

**Substance use disorder records**: The form correctly notes that "federal law prohibits further release of my information without my written consent and requires an additional specific form to be completed before the records are provided" for substance use disorder records. **Psychotherapy notes**: The form states "I know I need to sign a separate form to release any notes related to psychotherapy." This correctly reflects the separate authorization requirement under HIPAA for psychotherapy notes.

**Sensitive information disclosure**: The form includes a blanket statement that "the information released may include treatment for physical and mental illness, alcohol or drug use, AIDS (Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome) or HIV testing." There is no opt-out mechanism for sensitive categories.
