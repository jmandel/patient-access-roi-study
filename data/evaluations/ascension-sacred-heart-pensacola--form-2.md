The form uses the legacy "Sacred Heart Health System" branding rather than the current "Ascension Sacred Heart" name, and the filename (`031616-records-release-shcc.pdf`) suggests it dates from March 2016.

## 4. CONTENT DESIGN

**Length**: One page (appropriate for the content).

**Clarity and language**: The form mixes plain-language instructions with dense legal boilerplate. The top section (patient information, record type selection) is reasonably clear, but the bottom half packs heavy legal text into a small space. **Purpose options**: Three choices: "at the request of the patient," "for diagnosis/treatment purposes," and "other."

## 5. PATIENT-CENTEREDNESS

This form is **not a patient access form**. It is designed for a narrow clinical workflow: authorizing the transfer of records from an outside provider into Sacred Heart's Radiation Oncology department. The pre-printed destination address and department name make this clear. A patient trying to obtain their own records should use form-1 instead; this form would not serve that purpose.

- **No electronic delivery option**: The form does not mention electronic vs. paper format (unlike form-1, which explicitly offers this choice).
- **No HIPAA Right of Access language**: There is no reference to 45 CFR ¬ß 164.524 or patient access rights. This is somewhat expected given that the form is designed for provider-to-provider transfer, not patient self-access.
- **No EHI export mention**: No mention of electronic health information export or bulk data.
- **Social Security Number requested**: The form asks for the patient's "SOCIAL SECURITY NO," which is a privacy concern. SSN is not necessary for medical records transfer and creates an unnecessary data exposure risk for patients.
- **Scope options**: Patients can specify date ranges and record types, which is appropriate for a records transfer form.

## 6. COMPLIANCE

- **Authorization expiration**: "This authorization will expire one (1) year from the date of my signature." This is standard.
- **Revocation rights**: The form states patients can revoke in writing, with standard exceptions for reliance and insurance conditions. However, the revocation instructions say to provide "a written revocation to the provider from which records are requested in the box above" ‚Äî meaning the revocation goes to the *source* provider, not to Sacred Heart. This is technically correct for an inbound transfer authorization.
- **Redisclosure warning**: Present: "I understand that my Protected Health Information (PHI) that is used or disclosed under this Authorization may be subject to redisclosure by the recipient."
- **Liability release**: The form includes language relieving the releasor of "any responsibility or liability that may arise from the release or reproduction of such records." While common, this language is one-sided and may discourage patients from asserting their rights.
- **Witness requirement**: The form has a "Witness" signature line with a corresponding "Date" field. This adds a barrier for patients completing the form independently, as it implies a witness is required for the authorization to be valid.
