**Clarity**: The language is generally accessible, though it leans toward institutional/legal phrasing. **Record type options**: Section 6 offers specific categories (Visit Summary, ER Record, Lab, Radiology, Cardiac Tests, Billing) plus "Any & All Records" and an "Other" write-in. There is a separate checkbox and detailed instructions for authorizing radiology film release through Central Oregon Radiology Associates (CORA), adding complexity.

## 5. PATIENT-CENTEREDNESS

**Patient access vs. third-party release**: This is a **general-purpose authorization form** ‚Äî it handles both patient access requests and third-party releases through the same From/To checkbox mechanism. There is no dedicated patient-access pathway. The form states: *"This form can be used for records of St. Charles Health System or records of other health care providers"*, meaning it also functions as an incoming records request form.

**Scope options**: Good granularity. Patients can specify:
- Date range of services
- Specific record types (6 categories plus "Other")
- Specific St. Charles locations
- Sensitive record types requiring separate initials (substance abuse, HIV, mental health, genetic)

**Format options**: Limited. Section 8 offers only **Paper** and **CD**: *"Records Format (paper is the default if not marked): Paper / CD."* No electronic delivery (email, patient portal, secure link). No fax option for patients (fax noted elsewhere as restricted to provider-to-provider).

**Delivery options**: Only **U.S. Mail** and **Pick up**. No electronic delivery mechanism.

**Right of Access language**: The form does **not** reference HIPAA ¬ß 164.524 or the patient's right of access. It references federal/state regulations only in the context of copy fees: *"Standard copy fees will apply subject to federal and state regulations."*

**EHI Export awareness**: No mention of electronic health information export, USCDI, or bulk data access.

**Purpose options**: The form offers "Legal / Insurance / Personal / Continuation of Care / Other" ‚Äî the "Personal" option serves as the closest proxy for a patient self-access request.

## 6. COMPLIANCE

**Wet-ink signature requirement**: The form explicitly states **"MUST BE WET-INK SIGNED"** next to the signature line (section 9). This effectively prevents fully electronic submission and requires printing, signing, and physical delivery (mail or in-person). This is a notable barrier to digital access.

**Photo ID check**: The office-use section includes a *"Photo ID checked"* checkbox, suggesting in-person identity verification is expected or at least standard practice.

**Copy fees**: The form states twice that *"Standard copy fees will apply subject to federal and state regulations"* but does not specify amounts. This is appropriately vague ‚Äî it defers to the regulatory framework rather than setting its own fee schedule.

**Expiration**: *"Unless I revoke this authorization, it will expire on the following date or event: ______. If an expiration date is not specified, this authorization will expire one year from the date it is signed."* A one-year default expiration is reasonable and standard.

**No treatment conditioning**: The form correctly states: *"No determination about treatment, payment, enrollment, or eligibility for benefits will be based on whether or not I sign this authorization form."*

**Re-disclosure warning**: Includes appropriate language: *"I understand that federal confidentiality rules will not protect the medical information that I have authorized to be released, if it is released again by the organization or person that receives it."*

**Minor consent protections**: Instructions note Oregon-specific minor consent rules: *"No drug and alcohol treatment records of a minor who is 14 years old or older, nor medical records of any type of a minor who is 15 years old or older, may be released without the minor's written authorization if the minor is self-consented to the treatment associated with the records."*

**Rejection right**: *"St. Charles reserves the right to reject this authorization form if the legal authority of the representative cannot be validated."*
